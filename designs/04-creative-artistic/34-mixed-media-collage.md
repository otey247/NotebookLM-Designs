# Design 34: Mixed Media Collage

**Category:** Creative & Artistic  
**Style Tags:** collage, mixed-media, handcrafted, layered, personal, tactile  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Personal brand presentations, creative portfolio showcases, arts education content, brand origin story decks, and any presentation that values authentic human handwork over digital polish. Ideal for artists, creative directors, and makers.

---

## Visual Direction

Layered cut-paper aesthetics, magazine clipping compositions, visible tape and staple elements as design features, texture overlays from different physical materials, intentional misregistration suggesting analog production, and typography mixing from different printed sources. The slide looks like it was physically assembled on a studio table.

## Background & Texture

Aged newsprint (#E8E0D0) or craft paper brown (#C4A87A) as the base layer. Multiple paper texture layers overlaid at varying opacities — white bond paper, colored construction paper, torn newspaper edges. The background itself is a collage of material textures rather than a single surface.

## Typography

The defining feature: mixed type sources simulating cut-and-paste assembly. Large magazine headline letters mixed with typewriter body text mixed with handwritten annotations mixed with rubber stamp labels. Each text element appears to come from a different source. No single typeface family — the diversity of type sources IS the system.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Natural craft paper tan (#C4A87A) — dominant background tone
- **Secondary:** Aged newsprint gray-white (#E8E0D0) — paper layer color
- **Accent 1:** Vivid cutout colors — bright reds, yellows, and blues from magazine clipping sources
- **Accent 2:** Marker highlight orange (#FF8C00) or yellow (#FFD700)
- **Neutral:** Ink black (#1A1A1A) — rubber stamps, pen marks, handwritten text

## Layout & Composition

Elements are layered and overlapping, positioned as if physically placed and moved until the composition felt right. Slight rotation on many elements (1–5 degrees) simulates physical placement. Tape strip textures at element edges. Staple graphics holding groups of elements together. The composition follows internal logic of the collage rather than grid alignment.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Torn paper edge effects on photo and paper cutouts. Visible tape strips across element edges. Staple graphics. Washi tape patterns. Magazine image fragments pasted at angles. Pen marks, circles, and arrows connecting related elements. Rubber stamp text labels ("APPROVED", "CUT HERE", section numbers). Paper clip graphics. Torn border edges on the slide itself.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Large display letters in mixed cut-and-paste styles, torn paper edge around the slide, a photography magazine clipping as hero image, handwritten subtitle annotation.
- **Content Slide:** Multiple overlapping paper layer sections, each with a different material texture, typewriter body text on one layer, handwritten notes on another, rubber stamp labels.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Sequential steps on separate paper scrap effects, connected by hand-drawn arrows and pen lines, each with different paper color.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Statistics hand-lettered on a bright colored paper cutout, simple bar chart drawn on graph paper scrap, tape holding it to the background.
- **Closing Slide:** Collage of photography fragments and text clippings creating a mosaic of the key messages, handwritten closing statement in the center.

## Tone & Mood

Tactile, personal, and authentically handcrafted. The tone communicates: a human being assembled this, and that effort and presence shows. There is warmth in the imperfection and creative courage in the artistic risk. Audiences feel invited into a creative process rather than presented at.

## Avoid

- Clean digital alignment of elements
- Single typeface consistency
- Smooth, texture-free surfaces
- Corporate color palettes
- Geometric precision
- Any element that removes the sense of physical assembly

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in mixed media collage style. Use an aged craft paper and newsprint background with multiple overlapping paper texture layers, magazine headline letters in mixed sizes and styles for the title, typewriter body text on a separate overlapping paper layer, torn edge effects on all photo and paper cutout elements, visible washi tape strips holding elements in place, hand-drawn pen arrows and circles connecting related content, rubber stamp labels in ink black, and slight rotations (1–5 degrees) on multiple elements to suggest physical placement. The feel should be tactile, authentically handmade, and creatively personal — slide design as studio collage.
