# Design 31: Brutalist Design

**Category:** Creative & Artistic  
**Style Tags:** brutalism, anti-design, raw, confrontational, system-fonts, post-internet  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** Experimental presentations, design critique talks, counter-culture creative pitches, design conference provocations, and contexts where disrupting conventional visual expectations IS the message. Ideal for art directors, experimental designers, and critical theorists.

---

## Visual Direction

Raw HTML aesthetics, exposed structural bones, deliberate violation of design conventions, system fonts at extreme scales, broken layout as intentional choice, anti-design philosophy made tangible. Brutalism in presentation design communicates that the content is too important and too real to hide behind polished aesthetics. The ugliness is the honesty.

## Background & Texture

Pure white (#FFFFFF) — the default HTML page background. No texture, no atmosphere, no effort at visual warmth. Sometimes a jarring solid color block (bright yellow, vivid green, raw cyan) is used as a full-bleed background specifically to unsettle expectations.

## Typography

System fonts used intentionally: Arial, Times New Roman, Courier New, Comic Sans (yes, deliberately). Mixed at extreme scale contrasts. Type may bleed off the slide edge. Underlined text (like hyperlinks). Bold in mid-sentence arbitrarily. ALLCAPS for no reason then lowercase suddenly. The rules of typographic hierarchy are known and deliberately broken.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) — raw HTML background
- **Secondary:** Pure black (#000000) — system-default text
- **Accent 1:** Hyperlink blue (#0000EE) — unvisited link color
- **Accent 2:** Visited purple (#551A8B) — used link color
- **Neutral:** Border gray (#808080) — default HTML element borders

## Layout & Composition

The grid exists only to be broken. Elements placed at unexpected positions — top-right corner, bottom-left edge, bleeding off the slide. Visible HTML table borders used as composition tools. Text overlapping images. Asymmetry so extreme it initially reads as accidental. Elements sized by apparent whim (but actually by jarring contrast logic).

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Visible black borders around div-like boxes. Horizontal rules (the old HR tag). Blinking cursor-style elements. Checkboxes and form field aesthetics as decorative elements. Visible pixel-art icons. Low-resolution images without anti-aliasing. Scrollbar mockups on the slide edge. The aesthetic vocabulary of default browser rendering.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** System font headline at extreme scale bleeding off right edge, a blue hyperlink-styled subtitle, visible black borders around the slide content area, a "WELCOME" in Comic Sans somewhere.
- **Content Slide:** Table-formatted content with visible border cells, mixed font size paragraphs, underlined headings, a jarring color background zone.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered list in Times New Roman at large scale, no other visual treatment — the list IS the design.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Raw data table with default HTML table borders, a low-resolution bar chart image, no chart title or axis labels (they're in the table headers).
- **Closing Slide:** Single sentence in system font at 400% scale bleeding off all edges. Nothing else.

## Tone & Mood

Confrontational, post-ironic, and intellectually provocative. The tone communicates that design conventions are a cage and this presentation has chosen to step outside it. There is sophisticated awareness behind the apparent naivety. Audiences who get it feel initiated; audiences who don't feel productively unsettled.

## Avoid

- Any attempt at conventional design elegance
- Consistent typographic hierarchy
- Deliberate color harmony
- Smooth gradients or soft visual elements
- Following any design school rule without consciously breaking it
- Trying to make it "look good" in conventional terms

## Example Prompt

> Design a brutalist presentation slide using deliberate anti-design principles. Use a pure white HTML-default background, system fonts (Times New Roman for headlines at extreme scale, Comic Sans for one element, Courier for body text), pure black element borders, hyperlink blue (#0000EE) as the accent color, a table with visible grid borders for content organization, text bleeding off the slide edge, uneven element sizes and placement that violates conventional grid logic, and a horizontal rule as a section divider. The feel should be raw, confrontationally ugly-by-choice, and intellectually provocative — anti-design as design philosophy.
