# Design 33: Abstract Expressionism

**Category:** Creative & Artistic  
**Style Tags:** abstract, expressionism, Rothko, gestural, emotional, color-field  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** Creative leadership presentations, innovation keynotes, cultural transformation talks, arts organization presentations, and any context where the emotional register of the presentation should be as carefully designed as the logical argument. Ideal for visionary leaders and creative industry professionals.

---

## Visual Direction

Mark Rothko color field meditation, Franz Kline gestural black ink calligraphy, Helen Frankenthaler color wash spontaneity — emotion conveyed through color relationships and mark-making rather than literal representation. The slide is a felt experience before it is a read experience. Content floats within or against large emotional color fields.

## Background & Texture

Large gestural color field backgrounds built from two or three deeply saturated but harmonically related colors bleeding into each other at their edges. Visible brushwork texture in the color field — this should feel painted, not digitally smooth. The paint quality IS the texture.

## Typography

Spare, carefully considered type that respects the painting it inhabits. A single serif or sans-serif in medium weight. Type appears to float within or against the color field rather than sit on top of it. White or cream type on dark fields. Dark type on light fields. Never more than two or three lines. The fewer words, the greater the emotional weight each word carries.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Rothko ochre (#C8922A) — warm deep yellow, primary color field
- **Secondary:** Deep sienna (#7A3A1A) — warm dark, border and secondary field
- **Accent 1:** Prussian blue (#1A3A5C) — cool counterpoint to warm primaries
- **Accent 2:** Burnt umber (#6B3A2A) — deep warm grounding
- **Neutral:** Bone white (#F5EEE0) — type color, light field element

---

*Note: Color palette is a suggestion — the defining feature is two large fields of emotionally resonant, deeply saturated harmonious colors.*

## Layout & Composition

The composition is the color field relationship. Large rectangular zones of color stacked horizontally (Rothko's signature) or flowing diagonally (more Frankenthaler). Text appears in one zone, typically the lighter or lower one. There is no conventional grid — the color field proportions ARE the compositional system.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

The gestural brushwork of the color field is the primary graphic element. Optional: a single Franz Kline-style bold black ink brushstroke as a graphic anchor. Soft edge between color zones (the "bleed" is intentional — no hard edges). Occasional drip or flow mark suggesting the physical act of painting. Nothing representational.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Two large stacked color fields occupying the full slide, presentation title in large bone-white type floating in the lower field, organization name in small type below.
- **Content Slide:** Color field background with text flowing in a single small column in the calmer zone of the painting, maximum 50 words.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Each process stage associated with a different color field temperature — cool for early stages, warm for final outcomes. Transitions shown through color shifts.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** A single statistic or finding in very large type positioned against the color field — the data becomes an emotional as much as informational statement.
- **Closing Slide:** Pure color field. No text. The final emotional statement.

## Tone & Mood

Visceral, contemplative, and feeling-first. The tone communicates that some truths are felt before they are understood, and the most important ideas have an emotional dimension that purely informational design cannot access. Audiences experience something rather than just receiving information.

## Avoid

- Literal or representational illustration
- Multiple competing information elements per slide
- Bright primary colors without gestural depth
- Digital-smooth gradients (the painted texture is essential)
- Conventional grid or layout structure
- More than one major graphic element per slide

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in abstract expressionist style inspired by Mark Rothko and Franz Kline. Create two large stacked rectangular color fields taking the full slide surface — a deep Prussian blue field in the upper 60% and a warm Rothko ochre field in the lower 40% — with a soft, slightly blurred bleed between them that suggests painted edges rather than a clean digital border. Apply a visible brushwork texture to both fields. Float a single line of bone-white serif text in the lower color field. Add one gestural black ink brushstroke mark as a Franz Kline-inspired graphic anchor. The feel should be visceral, emotionally resonant, and contemplative — slide design as abstract painting.
