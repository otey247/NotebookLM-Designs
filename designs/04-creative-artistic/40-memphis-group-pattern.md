# Design 40: Memphis Group Pattern

**Category:** Creative & Artistic  
**Style Tags:** Memphis, 1980s, postmodern, Sottsass, pattern, maximalist  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Creative agency pitches, design conference presentations, branding presentations for playful brands, retro-future product launches, and any context where celebrating the aesthetics of postmodern design is itself the message. Ideal for design industry audiences.

---

## Visual Direction

Ettore Sottsass and the Memphis Group's radical 1980s postmodern design — squiggly lines as surface patterns, confetti dot fields, checkerboard sections, clashing geometric shapes in wrong-for-each-other pastel and vivid color combinations, deliberately anti-functional decoration. Memphis rejected the austerity of good design and replaced it with exuberant pattern, color, and surface pleasure.

## Background & Texture

Memphis pattern backgrounds: the squiggle pattern (thin repeating wave lines on solid color), confetti dots (random colored dots on solid ground), checkerboard in unexpected color combinations, or diagonal stripe patterns. Background IS the Memphis identity — pure surface pleasure with no functional pretense.

## Typography

Bold, clean geometric sans-serif in high-contrast combinations — black on pastel, white on vivid, or vivid on pastel. Memphis typography is clear and readable (the pattern does the expressive work) but positioned within or over the patterns in confident, large scale. Occasional Memphis lettering with the characteristic thick stroke geometric style.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Memphis pastel pink (#FF9EC4) — the defining Memphis pastel
- **Secondary:** Memphis turquoise (#00BFBF) — cool complement to pink
- **Accent 1:** Memphis yellow (#FFE033) — the lemon-yellow of the era
- **Accent 2:** Memphis cobalt (#2255BB) — structural color contrast
- **Neutral:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) and pure black (#000000) — anchors amid the pattern chaos

## Layout & Composition

Memphis composition uses pattern surfaces as primary organizational zones. Different sections of the slide use different Memphis patterns — the visual change in pattern (squiggle to checkerboard to dots) creates sectional differentiation without conventional headers. Typography sits confidently within pattern zones. Geometric shapes (circles, triangles, zigzag forms) appear as decorative-structural elements throughout.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

The Memphis design vocabulary: squiggle line patterns, confetti dots, checkerboards, half-moon shapes, zigzag lines as decorative elements, Memphis laminate-style surface patterns on shapes, pastel-colored geometric objects as decorative anchors, zebra stripe patterns, and the characteristic Memphis color combinations (pastels + vivids in unexpected pairing).

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Memphis squiggle pattern background, geometric shapes as compositional elements, bold headline in clean sans-serif on a solid color band, confetti dot zone for a title sub-element.
- **Content Slide:** Two or three Memphis pattern zones creating content sections, bold section labels on each pattern surface, body text in clean sans on the least visually active zone.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Sequential steps as Memphis-styled geometric objects (checkerboard circles, squiggle-filled rectangles) connected by zigzag lines.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Data bars styled with Memphis patterns as fills, bold numbers as labels, pattern-filled background zones.
- **Closing Slide:** Full Memphis pattern extravaganza — all the patterns, all the colors, maximum surface pleasure.

## Tone & Mood

Joyfully excessive, nostalgic-future, and playfully rebellious against good taste. The tone communicates: rules are for breaking, pleasure is a valid design goal, and the 1980s were right about some things. Audiences feel the specific joy of aesthetic permission to be excessive. Design students, creative professionals, and culture-literate audiences love this.

## Avoid

- Muted or desaturated colors
- Minimal white space
- Conventional typographic restraint
- Single pattern consistency (Memphis mixes all its patterns)
- Functional-only design thinking
- Anything that resembles "good design" by conventional standards

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in Memphis Group style inspired by Ettore Sottsass's 1980s postmodern design movement. Use a Memphis squiggle line pattern as the dominant background section alongside a confetti dot zone and a checkerboard pattern zone, all in the Memphis pastel and vivid color palette (pink, turquoise, yellow, cobalt). Add geometric decorative elements — pastel-colored half-moon shapes, zigzag divider lines, Memphis laminate-textured rectangles. Position bold clean sans-serif headline text confidently within the squiggle zone. Include confetti dots and geometric shapes as decorative-structural elements throughout. The feel should be joyfully excessive, pattern-saturated, and postmodern in its gleeful rejection of conventional design austerity.
