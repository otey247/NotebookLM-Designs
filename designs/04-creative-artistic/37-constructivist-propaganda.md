# Design 37: Russian Constructivist

**Category:** Creative & Artistic  
**Style Tags:** constructivism, Soviet, El-Lissitzky, Rodchenko, diagonal, agitprop  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** Movement-building presentations, social change campaigns, product launches framed as revolutions, collective action advocacy, and design history talks. Ideal when the presenter wants to frame their work as a significant, historically resonant change from the status quo.

---

## Visual Direction

El Lissitzky, Rodchenko, and Mayakovsky poster aesthetics — bold diagonal compositions defying the horizontal convention, geometric shapes as functional symbols, the revolutionary palette of red and black on white, photomontage compositions layering photography with geometric forms, and worker-hero agitprop energy translated to contemporary context.

## Background & Texture

Pure white (#FFFFFF) — the Constructivists worked on clean surfaces. Occasionally black as a full section background. Red and black geometric shapes do the work of the background through compositional force, not surface treatment.

## Typography

Bold, geometric sans-serif or slab serif — heavy industrial weight. All-caps for maximum authority. Diagonal text setting at 30–45 degrees creates the characteristic Constructivist dynamism. Large scale variations within a single text element — first word enormous, subsequent words smaller, creating a typographic hierarchy that reads like a poster. Cyrillic-inspired letterform construction (rigid, geometric, powerful) applied to any alphabet.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Revolutionary red (#CC0000) — the defining accent color of the movement
- **Secondary:** Constructivist black (#0A0A0A) — type and geometric elements
- **Accent 1:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) — background and knockout type
- **Accent 2:** Industrial gray (#888888) — secondary geometric elements
- **Neutral:** (none — Constructivism does not do neutrality)

## Layout & Composition

Bold diagonal composition as the primary organizing principle. Elements set at 30–45-degree angles create dynamic tension across the slide surface. Large geometric shapes (triangles, circles, rectangles, bars) overlap at different angles. Photography placed at an angle within a geometric frame. Red and black geometric elements are load-bearing composition members, not decorative accents.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Large solid geometric shapes (triangle as arrow, circle as anchor, rectangle as platform). Diagonal rule lines cutting across the composition. Photomontage effects combining photography with geometric overlays. Bold rule bars at angles. Five-pointed star as an archetype of the era (used carefully, historically aware). Worker or figure silhouette in black at large scale.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Full composition of diagonal geometric shapes in red and black, white headline type set at diagonal angle within a bold red bar, worker silhouette or figure at large scale.
- **Content Slide:** Black background zone with white diagonal type headline, red geometric shape anchoring the composition, body text in white on black or black on white zone.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Diagonal progression arrows in red, each step in a geometric shape at increasing scale, suggesting growing momentum.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Bar chart elements styled as Constructivist geometric bars with diagonal typography, red highlighting key data point.
- **Closing Slide:** Single bold diagonal statement, large red geometric emphasis shape, white knockout type.

## Tone & Mood

Urgent, collective, and historically resonant. The tone communicates that this work is part of a larger transformation — that this is not just a presentation but a call to coordinated action. The energy is monumental and the stakes are high. Audiences feel the weight of potential collective impact.

## Avoid

- Soft or decorative visual language
- Horizontal-only composition
- Multiple accent colors beyond red and black
- Photographic backgrounds (use photomontage, not photography as background)
- Lowercase type
- Any element that suggests comfort rather than urgency

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in Russian Constructivist style inspired by El Lissitzky and Alexander Rodchenko. Use a white background with bold diagonal geometric elements in revolutionary red (#CC0000) and constructivist black, a large triangle pointing to the focal element, a bold rectangular red bar containing the headline in white all-caps type set at a 30-degree diagonal angle, a worker figure silhouette in black at large scale in one composition zone, diagonal rule lines cutting across the slide, and black and red overlapping geometric shapes creating dynamic visual tension. The feel should be urgent, boldly ideological, and compositionally dynamic — the visual language of revolutionary design.
