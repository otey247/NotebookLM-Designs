# Design 39: Dadaist Disruption

**Category:** Creative & Artistic  
**Style Tags:** Dada, disruption, chance, anti-rational, photomontage, absurdist  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** Creative thinking workshops, design conferences, innovation keynotes where conventional thinking needs to be disrupted, and presentations about radical creativity or systems change. Ideal for creative directors, innovation leaders, and design educators.

---

## Visual Direction

Tristan Tzara's chance poetry, Hannah Höch's photomontage scissors and glue, Duchamp's readymade logic — absurdist juxtaposition, randomness as method, anti-narrative that paradoxically communicates through what it refuses to be. The slide presents information through deliberate dislocation, forcing the audience to actively construct meaning rather than passively receive it.

## Background & Texture

Deliberately inconsistent: some zones pure white, others with newspaper clipping texture, some filled with a jarring solid color. The background itself refuses to be singular or stable. Multiple paper textures overlaid at different angles. No unified surface — a collage of backgrounds.

## Typography

The Dadaist typography experiment — letters cut from different sources, different sizes, different faces, combined at random angles. But the "randomness" is curated to communicate. Words scattered across the slide surface without conventional reading order — the reader must work to assemble meaning. Occasional normal type as contrast — the normalized appears bizarre next to the Dadaist.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Jarring unpredictability — what creates contrast and discomfort
- **Secondary:** Newspaper gray-white (#E0DDD8) — clipping source background
- **Accent 1:** Explosive intrusion color — vivid red (#CC0000) as an interruption
- **Accent 2:** Unexpected presence — vivid cyan (#00CCCC) from nowhere
- **Neutral:** (Dada has no neutrals — even gray is a statement)

## Layout & Composition

Chance operations inform the layout but with curated intent (like Cage's chance music — chance within a designed system). Elements placed at unexpected rotations, scales, and positions. Reading direction defied — some text reads bottom-to-top or right-to-left. The slide has no single focal point but multiple equal competing tensions. The composition creates productive disorientation.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Hannah Höch-style photomontage: body parts from different images combined into impossible figures. Printed text from different sources cut and pasted at angles. Ready-made objects (scissors icon, glue bottle graphic) as design elements. Rubber stamp marks interrupting images and text. Torn edges. Scotch tape visuals. Nonsense words or Dada poems as texture. 

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Title letters scattered across the slide from different type sources, assembled in reading order but barely — the audience must work to read it. A photomontage figure in the background.
- **Content Slide:** Content presented in at least three different typographic treatments across the slide surface, with connecting arrows that lead to unexpected conclusions.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Sequential steps presented out of sequence, numbered non-consecutively, with the audience reconstructing the correct order as part of the experience.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Statistics presented in unexpected typographic scale and position, with Dadaist annotations questioning the meaning of the data.
- **Closing Slide:** A single absurdist statement that reframes everything before it. No conventional closing.

## Tone & Mood

Intellectually disruptive, playfully serious, and productive in its confusion. The tone communicates: our conventional ways of seeing and organizing information are constructs that can be set aside. Within the apparent chaos is a disciplined invitation to think differently. Audiences are productively destabilized.

## Avoid

- Conventional layout or composition logic
- Consistent typographic hierarchy
- Passive or polite visual energy
- Decoration for its own sake (Dada disrupts WITH intention, not randomly)
- Safe colors or predictable compositions
- Conclusions served without requiring audience engagement

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in Dadaist style inspired by Hannah Höch photomontage and Tristan Tzara chance poetry. Use a background combining multiple paper textures at different angles (newspaper clipping zones, pure white zones, solid color intrusion zones), title letters scattered across the surface in different typefaces and sizes assembled in reading order but barely so, a Hannah Höch-style photomontage figure combining body parts from different sources, connecting arrows leading to unexpected conclusions, rubber stamp mark interruptions over images and text, occasional vivid red and cyan color intrusions from unexpected positions, and torn paper edge effects throughout. The feel should be intellectually disruptive, productively disorienting, and serious about its playfulness.
