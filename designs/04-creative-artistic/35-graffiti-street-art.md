# Design 35: Graffiti & Street Art

**Category:** Creative & Artistic  
**Style Tags:** graffiti, street-art, urban, spray-paint, wildstyle, mural  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Community programs, urban planning presentations, youth engagement content, creative industry pitches, hip-hop cultural topics, and urban brand presentations. Ideal for organizations working in urban spaces and cultures.

---

## Visual Direction

Urban wall mural aesthetics — spray paint gradient fills, throw-up bubble letter styles, wildstyle interlocking typography, tag mark elements, drips and splatter as decorative features, and brick or concrete wall textures as background surfaces. The design channels the kinetic creative energy of urban public art.

## Background & Texture

Weathered concrete wall texture (#8A8A8A base with surface variation) or exposed brick pattern. Aged wall surface with layers of paint showing through scratches and weathering. Alternative: clean white wall surface as a "fresh canvas" for bold graphic elements. Surface texture should feel physical and urban.

## Typography

Wildstyle and throw-up inspired typography: bubble letters with 3D drop shadows, interlocking letterforms, thick outline strokes with fill colors, chrome-style metallic fill effects on select elements. Tags in a quick single-stroke style for secondary labels. Body text in a clean street-readable sans-serif for contrast. Spray paint drip effects below select headline letters.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Urban concrete gray (#7A7A7A) — wall background tone
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#F5F5F5) — fresh-surface spray paint first layer
- **Accent 1:** Electric blue spray (#0044FF) — primary wildstyle color
- **Accent 2:** Hot orange spray (#FF6600) — secondary energy color
- **Neutral:** Neon lime accent (#AAFF00) — sharp accent highlight

## Layout & Composition

Wall composition logic: elements are painted from large to small across the surface. A large mural-scale wildstyle headline anchors the composition. Secondary elements (character, decorative tags, fill patterns) radiate around the primary lettering. Arrow shapes direct movement across the wall surface. Spray fade effects at edges where colors meet the wall.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Spray paint fade effects (hard edge spray transitions to wall texture). Bubble letter outlines with multi-layer fill (black outline, fill color, white highlight). Drip effects from overloaded spray can simulation. Tag-style secondary marks as texture elements. Arrow shapes in spray paint. Background fill patterns (clouds, cross-hatch, diagonal lines) in muted graffiti fill style. Character sketch outline placeholder.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Full concrete or brick wall background, large wildstyle bubble letter headline, spray paint character placeholder (human figure outline), tag marks in corners, spray fade at slide edges.
- **Content Slide:** Concrete wall background, large readable headline in styled street lettering, content in clean sans-serif on a semi-transparent tag background, spray paint arrow pointing to key content.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Sequential numbered stages as throw-up style numbered circles, left-to-right wall composition, spray arrows connecting stages, drip effects beneath numbers.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Statistics rendered in large bubble letter style, supporting chart with spray paint aesthetic, urban context photography placeholder alongside.
- **Closing Slide:** Bold wildstyle closing statement, tag signature mark, "Keep it real" energy in the composition, spray fade at all edges.

## Tone & Mood

Kinetic, community-powered, and unapologetically urban. The tone communicates authentic roots in street culture and respect for the tradition of public art as democratic expression. There is creative pride and cultural identity in every element. Audiences from urban communities feel seen and respected.

## Avoid

- Polished corporate aesthetics
- Clean white backgrounds without surface texture
- Conventional typographic conventions
- Passive or timid visual energy
- Cultural appropriation without context (ensure the aesthetic serves genuine connection to urban communities)
- Generic "graffiti font" without understanding the visual vocabulary

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in graffiti and street art style. Use a weathered concrete wall texture background with aging and surface variation, a large wildstyle bubble letter headline in electric blue with thick black outline, white highlight stroke, and 3D shadow in orange, spray paint fade effects at the slide edges where colors transition to wall texture, drip effects beneath key letters from spray can overflow simulation, tag-style secondary marks in the corners as texture, a clean readable sans-serif for body content on a semi-transparent background panel, and directional spray paint arrows pointing to key content. The feel should be kinetic, urban, and authentically street-culture inspired.
