# Design 51: Scientific Research Poster

**Category:** Academic & Educational  
**Style Tags:** scientific, research-poster, IMRAD, peer-review, conference, evidence-based  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Academic conference presentations, research findings summaries, evidence-based program evaluation briefings, and scientific communication to both specialist and lay audiences. Ideal for researchers, scientists, and academic professionals.

---

## Visual Direction

Conference research poster sessions, peer-reviewed journal visual standards, and academic institution credibility markers. The design communicates methodological rigor, evidence quality, and institutional affiliation. Visual elements directly serve the scientific argument — IMRAD structure (Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion) governs the slide architecture.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) primary surface. Light blue-gray (#EEF2F7) for abstract and summary boxes. University or institution colors in the header banner. No decorative texture — scientific communication prioritizes signal over noise.

## Typography

Clean institutional serif or sans-serif matching the journal or institution standard. Bold for section headers (INTRODUCTION, METHODS, RESULTS). Regular weight for body. Italic for scientific terminology and Latin phrases (in vitro, p < 0.05). Small-cap labels for figure and table references. Author affiliations in smaller italic. Abstract in slightly smaller body type.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Institutional navy (#1A3A6A) or university brand color — header and structure
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — body background
- **Accent 1:** Positive result green (#1A7A4A) — significant findings, positive outcomes
- **Accent 2:** Neutral blue (#4472C4) — informational elements, data visualization
- **Neutral:** Light gray (#E8ECF0) — abstract boxes, infoboxes, table backgrounds

## Layout & Composition

IMRAD poster structure adapted to landscape format: header with title and authors spanning full width, then two or three columns below with Introduction/Hypothesis left, Methods center, Results right, Conclusions spanning the bottom. Figures and tables embedded within the relevant section columns. Clear section header bars with rule lines.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Research figure placeholders with numbered labels (Figure 1, Figure 2) and detailed captions. Data tables with header rows, alternating row shading, and footnote references. Statistical notation (± SD, 95% CI, p < 0.05, n = 127). Flow diagrams for study design/methodology. Forest plots or other meta-analysis visualizations. Institution logo and funding acknowledgment.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Institution logo, study title in bold serif, authors and affiliations in italic, conference name and date, funding acknowledgment.
- **Content Slide:** Section header (INTRODUCTION), background literature context, study hypothesis in highlighted box, enrollment criteria table.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** CONSORT-style enrollment flowchart, treatment arms diagram, randomization schema, follow-up timeline.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Primary outcome figure (Kaplan-Meier, bar chart, or scatter plot), figure caption, statistical significance notation, findings summary box.
- **Closing Slide:** CONCLUSIONS section with numbered key findings, limitations box, future directions, references list, acknowledgments.

## Tone & Mood

Rigorous, honest, and evidence-first. The tone communicates scientific integrity — every claim is backed by visible evidence, every method is transparent, every limitation is acknowledged. The audience feels they can trust the analysis because the process is visible.

## Avoid

- Decorative or non-functional visual elements
- Conclusions that overstate the evidence
- Missing data sources or statistical context
- Non-standard color coding for data
- Small font sizes that make data unreadable
- Any element that could not appear in a peer-reviewed publication

## Example Prompt

> Design a scientific research poster presentation slide in standard academic conference format. Use a clean white background with an institutional navy header banner containing the study title, authors, and institution logo, IMRAD structure with clear bold section headers (INTRODUCTION, METHODS, RESULTS, CONCLUSIONS) using the poster's two-column grid, a primary results figure (bar chart or survival curve placeholder) with numbered figure caption and statistical notation (p < 0.05, 95% CI), a data table with alternating row shading and footnote references, a highlighted hypothesis box in the introduction section, and a funding acknowledgment footer. The feel should be rigorous, evidence-first, and methodologically transparent.
