# Design 60: TED Talk Keynote

**Category:** Academic & Educational  
**Style Tags:** TED, keynote, one-idea, simple, powerful, public-speaking  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Keynote presentations, public speaking events, conference talks, thought leadership moments, and any high-profile presentation where a single powerful idea needs to land with maximum impact on a large audience. Ideal for keynote speakers, executives making major announcements, and thought leaders.

---

## Visual Direction

TED and TEDx presentation slide philosophy — one idea per slide, photography takes precedence over text, maximum seven words per slide, bold typography stating the single idea, emotional story arc building to a revelation, and the "idea worth spreading" single-frame impact philosophy. The slide exists to enhance the spoken word, not replace it.

## Background & Texture

Full-bleed photography as the primary background for most content slides. Pure black (#000000) for dramatic transition slides. Clean white (#FFFFFF) for data or text-first moments. No texture — the photography IS the texture. TED red (#E62B1E) as the brand anchor on title and closing slides.

## Typography

Bold, clean sans-serif — Helvetica Neue, Inter, or similar. Maximum seven words per slide as a hard rule. Type in white on dark or photography backgrounds. Type in dark on light backgrounds. Large scale — if the text is small enough to be a caption, it's too small. One typographic level only — no headline + body text hierarchy.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** TED red (#E62B1E) — brand color for title, closing, and accent moments
- **Secondary:** Pure black (#000000) — dramatic slide backgrounds
- **Accent 1:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — primary text color on dark backgrounds
- **Accent 2:** (none — photography provides all other color)
- **Neutral:** Warm dark gray (#333333) — for text-on-white moments

## Layout & Composition

Centered or slightly off-center single element per slide. Photography fills the entire background. Text appears in a zone where there is clear contrast with the photograph (often the lower third or upper third in an area of sky, shadow, or plain surface). No information competing with the single idea. The rule: if you can put anything else on this slide, something is wrong.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Full-bleed photography (the primary graphic element). TED "x" logo mark in the title corner. Single large statistic as a full-slide graphic element ("18 minutes changed my life"). Emotional close-up photography that connects to the talk's human dimension. Occasional simple diagram — but only if it communicates what words cannot. No icons, no decorative elements, no background illustrations.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** TED red background, speaker name in bold white, talk title in large white, conference name and date below. The red is the brand moment.
- **Content Slide:** Full-bleed emotional photography, single bold white phrase in the high-contrast zone of the image, maximum 7 words.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** One word or concept per slide, delivered sequentially — the transitions between simple slides create the narrative momentum.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** One single statistic at maximum scale on a dark background ("90% of communication is nonverbal"). Source in very small text below.
- **Closing Slide:** TED red background, the single idea of the talk stated simply in large white, speaker name small below.

## Tone & Mood

Simple, powerful, and emotionally resonant. The tone communicates: this idea is important enough to stand alone. There is courage in the simplicity — the presenter trusts their words and voice more than the slide. Audiences receive the idea without distraction, and it lands with maximum weight.

## Avoid

- More than 7 words per slide
- Bullet point lists of any kind
- Multiple elements competing for attention
- Small photography or text-over-complex-photography
- Multiple colors on non-TED-brand slides
- Any element that suggests the slide is more important than the speaker

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in TED Talk keynote style. Use a full-bleed emotional photography background (human subject, strong contrast between subject and background), a single bold white phrase in Helvetica Neue or Inter at large scale positioned in the high-contrast lower third of the photograph, maximum 7 words, no other text elements. Alternatively, create a TED red (#E62B1E) brand moment slide with speaker name and talk title in large bold white centered on the red. The feel should be simple, powerful, and idea-first — one slide, one idea, maximum emotional and intellectual impact with minimum visual complexity.
