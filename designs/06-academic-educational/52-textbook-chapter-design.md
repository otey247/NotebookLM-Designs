# Design 52: Textbook Chapter Design

**Category:** Academic & Educational  
**Style Tags:** textbook, curriculum, scaffolded, learning-objectives, educational, structured  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Curriculum design presentations, corporate training programs, educational institution briefings, learning and development materials, and any structured educational content with clear learning objectives and scaffolded instruction. Ideal for instructional designers, educators, and L&D professionals.

---

## Visual Direction

University textbook chapter design aesthetics — learning objectives boxes at the chapter open, "Key Terms" sidebars, numbered worked examples, concept diagrams with labeled callouts, chapter summary sections, and the structured scaffolding of professional educational publishing. The design communicates: this information is organized to facilitate learning.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) as the primary surface. Publisher accent color (often teal, orange, or warm navy — choose one as the textbook's theme color) for all structural elements: chapter headers, learning objectives boxes, key term sidebars. Subtle horizontal rule line separations between sections.

## Typography

Publisher-standard serif for body text (Garamond, Palatino, or similar). Bold sans-serif for chapter titles and major section headers. Numbered section headers (1.1, 1.2, 2.3.1). "Key Terms" in bold italic when introduced. Caption text in smaller italic below figures and tables. Learning objectives in a bulleted list within a colored box.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Publisher accent (Textbook teal #008080 or Textbook orange #E87722) — all structural elements
- **Secondary:** White (#FFFFFF) — page surface
- **Accent 1:** Light tint of primary (at 15% opacity) — learning objectives boxes, sidebar backgrounds
- **Accent 2:** Secondary academic blue (#1E3A8A) — cross-reference boxes, supplementary content
- **Neutral:** Medium gray (#6B7280) — captions, footnotes, secondary information

## Layout & Composition

Single main content column with a narrower sidebar column for key terms, marginal notes, and cross-references. Chapter-opening spread format: full-width chapter title area at top with a chapter overview paragraph, followed by "Learning Objectives" box spanning the full width. Section numbers and headers organize the main content. Worked examples in bordered boxes.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

"Learning Objectives" box with bulleted list (After completing this section, you will be able to...). "Key Terms" sidebar boxes with bold term and definition. "Worked Example" numbered boxes with step-by-step solutions. "Check Your Understanding" question boxes. Figure and table placeholders with detailed captions and labels. Cross-reference notes ("See Section 4.3 for..."). Summary table at the end.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Chapter number and title in large bold sans-serif, chapter overview paragraph, "Learning Objectives" box with 3–5 bulleted objectives, chapter outline.
- **Content Slide:** Section number and header, body text in two-column layout with sidebar key term box, concept diagram with labeled callouts, cross-reference note.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered "Worked Example" box showing step-by-step process with each step in a bordered sub-box, final answer highlighted.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Labeled figure with complete caption, data table with header row in accent color, analysis questions below.
- **Closing Slide:** Chapter summary section, numbered key takeaways, review questions, key terms list, references.

## Tone & Mood

Pedagogically structured, encouraging, and clearly scaffolded. The tone communicates: this content is organized to help you succeed. Every structural element — learning objectives, worked examples, check questions — exists to support the learner. The audience feels supported rather than challenged.

## Avoid

- Unstructured, linear slide dumps
- Missing learning objectives or clear section organization
- Dense paragraphs without visual breaks
- Charts without explanation or analysis questions
- Any element that removes the instructional scaffolding
- Font sizes too small for classroom display

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in university textbook chapter format. Use a clean white background with a publisher teal accent color, a "Learning Objectives" box at the top in a light teal background tinted panel with bulleted learning outcomes, main body text in Garamond or Palatino in a primary column, a sidebar column with a "Key Terms" box highlighting a bold term with its definition, a labeled concept diagram with callouts in the main body, a numbered section header (2.3 Style Name and Sub-concept) in bold sans-serif, and a "Check Your Understanding" question box at the bottom. The feel should be pedagogically structured, scaffolded for learning, and visually organized for clear educational comprehension.
