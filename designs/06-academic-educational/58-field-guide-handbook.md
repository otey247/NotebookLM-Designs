# Design 58: Field Guide & Handbook

**Category:** Academic & Educational  
**Style Tags:** field-guide, handbook, utilitarian, Audubon, military-manual, rugged  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Training manuals, operational guides, onboarding programs, safety training, outdoor education, and how-to educational content where practical utility trumps visual sophistication. Ideal for field trainers, safety officers, military/law enforcement educators, and naturalist educators.

---

## Visual Direction

National Audubon Society field guides, U.S. Army FM manuals, and wilderness survival handbook aesthetics — identification key layouts, cross-section diagrams, numbered step-by-step procedures, warning/caution/danger boxes, quick reference tables, and the deliberately durable design of a book meant to survive field conditions.

## Background & Texture

Aged off-white (#F5EFE0) or very light olive (#F0EFE5) — the color of quality printed field guide paper. Subtle paper grain. Optional olive or tan cover page treatment for section dividers. The design should feel printable and functional, not digital-native.

## Typography

Clean, highly legible condensed sans-serif for body text (Helvetica Condensed, Myriad Pro Condensed) — maximum information density while maintaining legibility in field conditions. Bold for step numbers and warning headers. Italic for species names and technical terminology. Caption text in smaller condensed. Section headers in bold all-caps.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Olive green (#5C6B3A) — primary field color, section headers
- **Secondary:** Warm off-white (#F5EFE0) — page background
- **Accent 1:** Safety orange (#E87722) — DANGER/WARNING/CAUTION boxes
- **Accent 2:** Deep tan (#8B6347) — NOTICE/INFORMATION boxes, borders
- **Neutral:** Dark brown (#2A1F0A) — body text, very warm near-black

## Layout & Composition

Information-dense but clearly structured. Two or three columns of body text with narrow margins for compactness. Identification key format for classification content: taxonomic hierarchy with indented sub-categories. Numbered procedures in a bordered box. Warning/Caution/Danger/Notice boxes in distinct colors. Quick reference table spanning the full width. Illustration with labeled callouts alongside the text description.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

DANGER boxes (red/orange bordered with DANGER header and skull or warning icon). CAUTION boxes (yellow/amber). NOTE boxes (olive green). Numbered procedure steps in bordered boxes with step numbers in bold circles. Cross-section diagrams with detailed labeled callouts. Species or object identification silhouettes. Distribution maps or habitat maps. Quick reference summary table. "Field Notes" margin boxes for supplementary observations.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Field guide cover format — olive background, bold condensed title in white, edition and author information, regional scope statement, relevant silhouette illustration.
- **Content Slide:** Two-column identification format — illustration with labeled callouts left, identification criteria and distinguishing features list right, quick reference stats table.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Step-by-step procedure in numbered bordered boxes, each step numbered with bold circle, procedure title in bold header, warning box inserted where appropriate.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Quick reference comparison table (species, habitat, season, size, distinguishing feature columns), distribution map, measurement reference chart.
- **Closing Slide:** Quick Reference Card format — most critical information condensed to the most compact possible format for field use.

## Tone & Mood

Practically authoritative, safety-conscious, and no-nonsense. The tone communicates: this information could save your life or help you succeed in the field. There is no room for aesthetic indulgence when the function is survival and operational success. Audiences feel equipped and prepared.

## Avoid

- Decorative visual elements without functional purpose
- Small font sizes that would be unreadable in field conditions
- Complex layouts that slow information retrieval
- Warm or casual visual language
- Digital aesthetics (this should feel printable)
- Any element that reduces information density without improving comprehension

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in field guide and handbook style inspired by Audubon field guides and military FM manuals. Use a warm off-white background with subtle paper grain, olive green section headers in bold condensed sans-serif, a two-column identification layout with a detailed labeled diagram on the left and identification criteria list on the right, a safety orange DANGER warning box with bold header and numbered caution points, a quick reference comparison table with olive header row, numbered procedure steps in bordered boxes with bold circle step numbers, and a distribution map. The feel should be practically authoritative, information-dense, and durably functional — designed for use in the field, not for looking at in an office.
