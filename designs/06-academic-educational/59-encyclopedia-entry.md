# Design 59: Encyclopedia & Reference Design

**Category:** Academic & Educational  
**Style Tags:** encyclopedia, reference, Britannica, comprehensive, authoritative, two-column  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Knowledge base presentations, comprehensive documentation overviews, wiki-style content reviews, and any presentation that needs to communicate breadth, comprehensiveness, and authoritative coverage of a topic. Ideal for knowledge management teams, reference publishers, and educators.

---

## Visual Direction

Encyclopaedia Britannica, Wikipedia featured article, and reference book entry aesthetics — alphabetical tabs, entry headers, "See Also" cross-references, thumbnail images with captions, infobox tables, table of contents sections, and the specific visual authority of a comprehensive reference work.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) — reference works prioritize maximum readability. Very subtle cream (#FFFEF8) warmth may be used for printed reference aesthetics. Multi-column layout with visible column rule lines. Thin horizontal rules as section separators.

## Typography

Classic reference serif (Times New Roman, Century Schoolbook, or Garamond) for body text in the traditional two-column format. Bold for entry headings and sub-entry labels. Small caps for cross-reference labels ("SEE ALSO:", "RELATED ENTRIES:"). Body text at reference density — small but readable, two columns. Infobox header in a contrasting color band.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Reference navy (#1A3A6A) — entry headers, infobox headers, section rule accents
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — page surface
- **Accent 1:** Infobox gold/tan (#C4B07A) — infobox header background
- **Accent 2:** Cross-reference blue (#4472C4) — See Also links, cross-reference text
- **Neutral:** Light gray (#D8D8D8) — column rules, table borders, horizontal separators

## Layout & Composition

Classic encyclopedia two-column body layout. Entry title as full-width header above the columns. Right-aligned infobox (narrow column) for the key facts about the entry subject. Table of contents section for longer entries. "See Also" and "References" sections at the bottom. Alphabetical guide letters at the top or side margin. Page header with volume and letter range.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Infobox table: entry name in colored header, thumbnail image in the box, key facts in label: value format, colored header band. Alphabetical tabs along the edge. Entry number or page reference. Cross-reference text in blue italic. Footnote superscripts in the body text. Section sub-headers in bold small caps. Thumbnail photographs with captions below. "Featured Article" star badge if applicable.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Reference work cover format — encyclopaedia name, volume number, letter range (N–Q), edition year, publisher name.
- **Content Slide:** Entry title as full-width header, infobox with thumbnail and key facts right-aligned, two-column body text with introduction paragraph, bold sub-entry headers.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Historical development entry format — chronological sub-sections as reference entry headings (Early Period, Development, Modern Applications), each with body paragraph.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Reference infographic — population table, statistical comparison chart, geographic distribution map — with reference book styling.
- **Closing Slide:** "See Also" section with cross-reference list, "References" section with numbered bibliography in reference format, "External Links" section.

## Tone & Mood

Comprehensively authoritative, neutrally presented, and reliably factual. The tone communicates: this is the definitive reference on the subject, compiled with care for accuracy and completeness. There is intellectual comfort in the comprehensiveness — the reference work has covered all the essential ground.

## Avoid

- Opinion or advocacy (reference works are descriptive, not prescriptive)
- Inconsistent entry format
- Missing cross-references to related topics
- Photography without proper caption and credit
- Informal typography or casual tone
- Incomplete coverage of the subject

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in encyclopedia and reference design style. Use a clean white background, classic two-column body text in a reference serif (Times New Roman or Century Schoolbook), a bold entry title as a full-width header above the columns, a right-aligned infobox with a navy header band (entry name), thumbnail image, and key fact label:value pairs within it, bold small-caps sub-entry headers within the columns, cross-reference text in blue italic ("See Also: related entries"), footnote superscripts in the body, a thin column rule between the two text columns, and a "See Also" and "References" section at the bottom. The feel should be comprehensively authoritative, neutrally encyclopaedic, and definitively reference-grade.
