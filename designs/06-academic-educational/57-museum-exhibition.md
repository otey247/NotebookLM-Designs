# Design 57: Museum Exhibition Design

**Category:** Academic & Educational  
**Style Tags:** museum, exhibition, curatorial, gallery, Smithsonian, educational-panel  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Cultural institution presentations, museum program briefings, heritage and history content, public education presentations, and any content that needs to communicate with the authority and accessibility of a major museum exhibition. Ideal for curators, educators, and cultural institution professionals.

---

## Visual Direction

Smithsonian Institution, MoMA, and natural history museum exhibition panel design — informational panels with crisp photography, curatorial voice in captions, section wayfinding numbers, artifact spotlight treatments, object labels, and the specific combination of academic authority and visitor accessibility that characterizes great museum design.

## Background & Texture

Deep charcoal (#2A2A2A) or dark gallery gray (#1E1E1E) — museum exhibition walls. Warm gallery white (#FAFAF5) for text panel backgrounds. The contrast between dark walls and light panels mirrors the physical museum experience. Subtle linen or cotton texture for text panel backgrounds at very low opacity.

## Typography

Clean, highly legible sans-serif for all exhibition text — Gill Sans, Futura, or a contemporary humanist sans. Curated typographic scale: Exhibition title large, section title medium, object label small, caption smallest. Warm charcoal on white panels, white on dark backgrounds. Generous leading for accessibility. Object label format: [OBJECT NAME, Date, Material, Dimensions, Institution Collection].

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Gallery charcoal (#2A2A2A) — wall/background color
- **Secondary:** Warm panel white (#FAFAF5) — text panel surface
- **Accent 1:** Exhibition gold (#C4922A) — accent lines, title highlights, section markers
- **Accent 2:** Exhibition accent (varies per theme) — one specific accent per exhibition section
- **Neutral:** Medium warm gray (#8A8070) — captions, secondary labels, dividers

## Layout & Composition

Exhibition wall panel layout: large featured image taking 50–60% of the slide, text panel in the remaining space with headline, body description, and object label. Wayfinding number in a gold circle (01, 02, 03). "OBJECT SPOTLIGHT" framing for key artifacts. Section header bar at the top with exhibition and section name. Gallery quote in large italic running across the full width.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Object label cards (the small printed cards beside museum objects): Object Name in bold, Artist/Maker/Culture, Date, Medium, Dimensions, Credit Line. Wayfinding number badges in gold circles. Section header bars. Large-format photography with exhibition treatment (slightly cooled or warmed for cohesion). Gallery quote as a full-width pull element. "Did You Know?" sidebar boxes for supplementary information.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Exhibition title in large sans-serif on dark gallery background, gold accent rule line, exhibition date range, institution name, curating department.
- **Content Slide:** Featured object image left with object label below, explanatory text panel right with wayfinding number badge at top.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Exhibition journey arc — gallery sections as sequential labeled zones, each with thumbnail object image and one-sentence curatorial description.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Collection statistics presented as exhibition-style infographic (collection size, date range, geographic distribution, media types).
- **Closing Slide:** Exhibition credit panel — curators, funders, lending institutions, visitor information — on dark gallery background with gold accents.

## Tone & Mood

Curatorial, educational, and publicly accessible. The tone communicates: this has been carefully researched, thoughtfully presented, and you are invited to engage with it at your own pace. There is intellectual authority without condescension — the museum speaks to curious visitors of all backgrounds.

## Avoid

- Cluttered text panels (museum panels have strict word count limits)
- Technical jargon without explanation
- Photography without proper credit and context
- Inconsistent object label formatting
- Corporate visual language
- Bright or commercial color palettes

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in museum exhibition style. Use a deep charcoal gallery wall background, a warm white text panel taking the right third of the slide, the main featured object photograph taking the left 60%, a gold circle wayfinding number badge (02) at the top of the text panel, exhibition title and section name in clean Gill Sans or Futura sans-serif, object label below the photo (Object Name, Culture/Period, Date, Medium, Credit Line), a brief curatorial body paragraph in the text panel, and a gold accent rule line at the top of the text panel. The feel should be curatorial, educationally accessible, and institutionally authoritative — Smithsonian-quality exhibition design in presentation format.
