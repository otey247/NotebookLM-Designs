# Design 78: Interactive Fiction & Game UI

**Category:** Storytelling & Narrative  
**Style Tags:** interactive-fiction, RPG, game-UI, narrative-game, choice, adventure  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Training simulations, scenario-based learning, engagement-driven product demos, gamified corporate presentations, and any context where presenting choices and consequences creates stronger audience engagement than passive presentation. Ideal for L&D professionals, game designers, and interactive storytellers.

---

## Visual Direction

Disco Elysium dialogue interface, classic text adventure games, visual novel aesthetics, tabletop RPG rulebook design, and the video game tradition of choice-based narrative interfaces. The slide is a game screen: the player (audience member) sees a situation, a character speaking, choices to be made, and the consequences of previous choices.

## Background & Texture

Dark fantasy or noir atmospheric backgrounds appropriate to the game genre: dark stone dungeon, rainy city, ancient forest, or space station corridor — wherever the narrative is set. The environment texture communicates the world. Dark backgrounds allow the lighter UI elements to float in front of them.

## Typography

Game-appropriate typography: a bold display font for game title and major moments, a clean readable serif or sans for dialogue text (Trajan Pro for epic fantasy, Oswald for modern noir, etc.), monospace for system readouts or "stats" panels, and a handwritten style for "journal entry" or "clue" elements.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Deep atmospheric dark (#1A1A2E or appropriate game setting) — background
- **Secondary:** Dialogue panel off-white (#F0EDE8) — the dialogue box/text panel
- **Accent 1:** RPG gold (#C4922A) — highlighted choices, important items, success states
- **Accent 2:** Quest red (#CC2200) — danger, enemy, urgent choice, failure states
- **Neutral:** Stone gray (#5A6070) — inactive UI elements, background UI chrome

## Layout & Composition

Game screen layout: large atmospheric background environment occupying most of the slide. Dialogue box in the lower third — the classic RPG dialogue panel — with character portrait on the left and spoken dialogue on the right. Choice buttons or numbered options below the dialogue box. Character stats or inventory panel in the upper-right corner. Mini-map in one corner.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Dialogue panel with character portrait placeholder (left) and dialogue text (right). Choice buttons styled as RPG menu options (Option 1: Do this / Option 2: Do that). Character stats panel (HP, Level, Status). Mini-map indicator. Quest marker icons. Item pickup "loot" notification. Ambient environmental details in the background (torches, windows, stars). Atmosphere particle effects (rain, snow, leaves).

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Game title screen format — cinematic atmospheric background, game/presentation title in dramatic display font centered, "Press START / Click to Begin" styled prompt.
- **Content Slide:** Dialogue box with character speaking the key concept, choices below (A: Traditional approach / B: Novel approach / C: Ask for more information).
- **Process/Flow Slide:** RPG quest log format — "Main Quest: [Objective]", numbered quest steps as quest items to complete, progress tracking.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Stats panel expansion — performance metrics displayed as RPG character stats (Strength: Revenue, Intelligence: Innovation Score, Vitality: Employee Health).
- **Closing Slide:** "Quest Complete!" or end-of-chapter screen with score, achievements unlocked, and "Continue?" prompt.

## Tone & Mood

Engaging, participatory, and playfully serious. The tone communicates: you have agency here, and the choices matter. The game interface aesthetics transform passive presentation reception into active engagement. Audiences feel like players with genuine roles in the unfolding narrative.

## Avoid

- Non-interactive visual design that uses game elements only decoratively
- Missing the choice/consequence dynamic (game UIs exist to present choices)
- Confusion between genre aesthetic and mechanical convention
- Game UI elements without the narrative context that makes them meaningful
- Light backgrounds (game atmospheres are typically dark and immersive)
- Corporate visual elements that break the game world immersion

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide as a role-playing game dialogue screen. Use a dark atmospheric background (stone dungeon corridor, rainy city street, or ancient forest — appropriate to your narrative), a dialogue box panel in the lower third with a character portrait placeholder on the left and dialogue text in a clean serif on the right, two or three numbered choice buttons below the dialogue panel in RPG menu style, a character stats panel in the upper-right corner with level, HP, and status, a mini-map indicator in the upper-left, and atmospheric environmental detail in the background. All UI elements in gold/off-white on the dark background with RPG gold (#C4922A) for highlighted choices. The feel should be immersive, choice-driven, and narratively engaging — interactive fiction game UI applied to presentation design.
