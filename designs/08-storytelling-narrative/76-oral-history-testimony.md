# Design 76: Oral History & Personal Testimony

**Category:** Storytelling & Narrative  
**Style Tags:** oral-history, testimony, personal, first-person, transcript, human  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Community oral history projects, impact reporting, qualitative research findings, human-interest program presentations, and any content where first-person voice and human testimony are the most powerful evidence. Ideal for nonprofit communicators, community researchers, and storytellers.

---

## Visual Direction

StoryCorps interview aesthetics, oral history archive presentation design, transcript-style formatting, human-centered first-person testimony display — the visual language that says: this is a real person, these are their words, and their story matters. The design steps back to let the human voice fill the space.

## Background & Texture

Warm cream (#FFF8F0) or warm light gray (#F5F2EE) — comfortable reading surfaces. Very subtle paper texture at near-invisible opacity. The background is quiet because the human voice should be loud. Occasional warm photograph of the person being quoted or represented.

## Typography

The testimony itself in large, comfortable, readable italic serif — the typographic treatment of a meaningful quotation. Warm gray or deep charcoal color, not harsh black. The person's name in bold at the conclusion of their quote. Context label (date, location, age) in small light type below the name. All text given generous line spacing — oral testimony breathes.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Warm cream (#FFF8F0) — quiet background surface
- **Secondary:** Deep warm charcoal (#2A2520) — primary text
- **Accent 1:** Warm gold (#C4892A) — large quotation mark, emphasis rule, name accent
- **Accent 2:** Warm coral (#D4604A) — occasionally used for particularly powerful testimony moments
- **Neutral:** Medium warm gray (#9A9085) — date, location, and context labels

## Layout & Composition

Quotation as the primary visual element — in large italic serif, centered or left-aligned, with generous margins. Large decorative quotation mark in warm gold at the beginning. Person's name in bold below, right-aligned or centered. Context information below the name. Portrait photograph (if available) positioned in the upper or lower zone, treated with a warm tone. White space is the container for the human voice.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Large decorative opening quotation mark (") in warm gold. Attribution line beneath each testimony. Portrait photograph with warm tonal treatment. Simple horizontal rule in warm gold before or after the testimony. "RECORDED IN [LOCATION] · [DATE]" context stamp in small caps. Community or project logo mark. Testimony collection series number.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Project title in large warm serif, subtitle describing the collection scope, portrait of one featured community member, organization name, and collection date range.
- **Content Slide:** One extended testimony quote in large italic serif, attribution line, portrait photograph in the upper-right zone, location and date context.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Multiple brief testimony excerpts — three or four voices responding to the same question, showing the range of community experience.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Quantitative findings contextualized by one testimony excerpt — the human experience behind the number.
- **Closing Slide:** Final powerful testimony quote, acknowledgments of all participants, collection information, and how others can access the full archive.

## Tone & Mood

Human, respectful, and testimony-honoring. The tone communicates that every person quoted here deserves to be heard and that their words contain more truth than any summary could. There is deep respect in giving human voices this much visual space. Audiences feel connected to real people and real experiences.

## Avoid

- Decorative elements that compete with the testimony text
- Small font sizes for testimony (the words deserve to fill the space)
- Cold or corporate visual language
- Photography without the subject's consent and knowledge
- Paraphrasing testimony (use direct quotation only)
- Any visual treatment that makes the subject feel less than dignified

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in oral history testimony style. Use a warm cream background (#FFF8F0), a large decorative gold quotation mark in the upper-left corner, the testimony quote in large italic Garamond or Georgia at comfortable reading size with generous line spacing and warm charcoal color, the person's name in bold below the quote right-aligned in a warm gold accent, a context label (Recorded: Lagos, Nigeria · March 2024) in small warm-gray light type below the name, a warm-toned portrait photograph of the speaker in the upper-right zone, and a thin gold horizontal rule separating the quote from the attribution. The feel should be human, respectful, and testimony-honoring — oral history in presentation design.
