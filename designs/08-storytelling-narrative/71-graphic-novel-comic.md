# Design 71: Graphic Novel & Comic Panel

**Category:** Storytelling & Narrative  
**Style Tags:** comic, graphic-novel, sequential-art, panel-layout, speech-bubble, halftone  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Process explanation presentations, product demo storytelling, customer journey illustrations, onboarding sequences, and any content that benefits from sequential visual narrative. Ideal for product teams, educators, and anyone who wants to make complex processes engaging through sequential art.

---

## Visual Direction

DC and Marvel page layouts, Watchmen dense panel grids, graphic novel memoir aesthetics (Maus, Persepolis, Fun Home) — panels as compositional units, speech bubbles as dialogue delivery, thought bubbles for internal states, caption boxes for narrative voice, action lines for motion and impact, and the halftone printing aesthetic of physical comics.

## Background & Texture

Panel-based layout where the slide IS a comic page — multiple bordered panels of different sizes creating a page grid. Panel backgrounds can be white (interior), sky blue (outdoor), black (nighttime or dramatic), or colored fills for atmosphere. Halftone dot texture may overlay all panels for printing-process authenticity.

## Typography

Comic/graphic novel lettering: bold all-caps sans-serif in speech bubbles (Bangers, Komika Text, or similar), slightly smaller all-caps for thought bubbles, bold all-caps for caption boxes, massive outline-stroke letters for WHAM! POW! sound effects. ALL CAPS is non-negotiable in traditional comics.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Panel ink black (#1A1A1A) — panel borders, outlines, typography
- **Secondary:** Panel white (#FFFFFF) — interior panel backgrounds
- **Accent 1:** Action yellow (#FFD700) — sound effects, emphasis moments, star bursts
- **Accent 2:** Sky blue (#5CB8FF) — sky backgrounds, thought bubbles, water
- **Neutral:** Halftone pink (#FFB8B8) — Ben-Day dot skin tone rendering

## Layout & Composition

Comic page panel grid: 6–9 panels of varying sizes arranged in a page grid. One hero panel taking 40–50% of the slide (the splash panel or establishing shot). Smaller panels around it for detail and dialogue. Panel borders in heavy black ink. Gutters (white space between panels) of consistent width. An occasional "bleed" panel touching the slide edge for drama.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Speech bubbles with tail pointing to speaker. Thought bubbles with cloud form and circles leading to character. Narrative caption boxes (rectangular panels in upper or lower corners). Action lines radiating from impact points. Sound effect lettering (CRASH!, SLAM!, CLICK!) in oversized outlined type. Panel number circles in the corner. Halftone dot fills for skin tones and color fills.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Full-page splash layout — one large panel with the hero image and title lettered in comic format, speech bubble with tagline, smaller establishing panels around.
- **Content Slide:** Six-panel grid — each panel illustrating one concept point, speech bubbles or caption boxes for explanatory text.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Sequential panels reading left-to-right, top-to-bottom — each panel one step in the process, action lines showing progression between panels.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Data presented within a comic panel frame, a character reacting to the data in an adjacent panel with speech bubble commentary.
- **Closing Slide:** Final panel composition — hero character with speech bubble containing the key message, "THE END" in comic convention format.

## Tone & Mood

Engaging, visual-narrative, and accessible. The tone communicates: learning this process is an adventure, and we're going to tell it as a story. There is genuine joy in the sequential narrative format that makes complex processes inherently engaging. Audiences feel like readers in a story rather than recipients of information.

## Avoid

- Dense body text paragraphs (use speech bubbles and caption boxes only)
- Non-panel based layouts (every element must be within a panel)
- Mixed typographic styles outside the comic convention
- Weak action lines or lack of visual dynamism
- Monotone panel sizes without variation (vary panel sizes for visual rhythm)
- Photo-realistic illustration style (use comic/graphic novel art style)

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide as a comic book page. Use a six-panel grid with one hero splash panel taking the upper two-thirds, three smaller equal panels in the lower third. Panel borders in heavy black ink with consistent white gutters. Speech bubbles with bold all-caps text in the hero panel, action lines radiating from the central figure, caption box in the upper-left corner for narrative voice, sound effect lettering in the lower hero panel, halftone dot texture overlaid on all panels. The characters and scenes are in graphic novel ink-and-color style. The feel should be sequential-narrative, visually dynamic, and comic-page authentic.
