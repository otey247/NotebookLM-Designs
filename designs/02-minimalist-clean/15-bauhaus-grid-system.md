# Design 15: Bauhaus Grid System

**Category:** Minimalist & Clean  
**Style Tags:** Bauhaus, constructivist, grid, primary-shapes, functional, Gropius  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Design system presentations, process documentation, design education, and any context where form-follows-function principles should be demonstrated through the design itself. Ideal for designers, architects, and systems thinkers.

---

## Visual Direction

Bauhaus school design principles — form follows function, primary shapes as the universal vocabulary, primary colors as the emotional palette, mathematical grid structure, functional typography. The aesthetic is simultaneously rational and expressive — geometric but not cold, primary but not childish. Every element is purposeful and every purpose is expressed visually.

## Background & Texture

Pure white (#FFFFFF) — the Bauhaus believed in clean surfaces as the appropriate ground for functional design. No texture, no atmospheric suggestion. Occasional light gray (#F2F2F2) background zones for content differentiation. The grid lines of the underlying structure may be subtly visible.

## Typography

Universal (Bauhaus) typeface or its modern equivalents — Futura, Avant Garde, Century Gothic. Clean geometric letterforms where circles are circles and lines are lines. Bold weight for headlines, regular for body. No italic — the Bauhaus rejected decorative typographic variation. Type used at systematic scale relationships: 48/32/16/12.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Bauhaus Red (#CC0000) — one of three primary colors, used for key emphasis
- **Secondary:** Bauhaus Blue (#1A3A8C) — structural elements, secondary sections
- **Accent 1:** Bauhaus Yellow (#F0C800) — highlights, feature accents, background zones
- **Accent 2:** Pure Black (#000000) — typography and geometric anchors
- **Neutral:** Pure White (#FFFFFF) — background and knockout elements

## Layout & Composition

Strict geometric grid. Composition built from primary shapes — rectangles, circles, and triangles — arranged by proportion and mathematical relationship. Bold horizontal and vertical divisions create zones. Color fills large zones rather than appearing as small accents. Overlapping circles and rectangles create visual energy through geometric interaction. Diagonal elements (from the De Stijl / constructivist influence on Bauhaus) punctuate the primarily orthogonal system.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Primary geometric shapes as both structural and decorative elements — a large solid circle as a section anchor, a rectangle containing text, triangular pointer arrows. No representational illustration. Photography if used appears in strict geometric crops (circles, squares). Thick rule lines in primary colors. Nested shapes (circle within square, triangle within circle) as conceptual icons.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Large rectangle in Bauhaus Blue taking top third, white headline type inside. Middle zone: large Bauhaus Red circle with title or number. Bottom zone: white with body text in black.
- **Content Slide:** Three-column grid with colored header bars (red, blue, yellow) above each column, black body text below, geometric shape anchors.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered circles (1, 2, 3, 4) in primary colors, connected by thick lines, labels below each in Futura.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Bauhaus-colored bar charts with geometric precision, bold number labels, primary color coding for categories.
- **Closing Slide:** Large Bauhaus Yellow rectangle as background, black bold type, single red circle accent in corner.

## Tone & Mood

Rational, principled, and energetically geometric. The tone communicates systematic thinking and design confidence. There is playful energy in the primary color interactions that prevents the rationalism from feeling cold. Audiences feel they are experiencing a design philosophy, not just a presentation.

## Avoid

- Soft colors or gradients
- Decorative typography
- Representational illustration
- Irregular or organic shapes
- Multiple typeface families
- Atmospheric texture or photography without geometric cropping

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in Bauhaus style inspired by the Dessau school principles. Use a pure white background, Futura or Century Gothic as the sole typeface, primary shapes (rectangles, circles, triangles) as both structural and decorative elements, and the Bauhaus primary palette (red, blue, yellow, black, white). Create bold geometric zones using large solid color rectangles as section anchors, thick primary-color rule lines as structural dividers, numbered circles in primary colors as sequence indicators, and geometric crops for any photography placeholders. The feel should be rationally constructive, energetically geometric, and principled — form following function expressed through pure geometry.
