# Design 19: White Space Editorial

**Category:** Minimalist & Clean  
**Style Tags:** white-space, luxury-editorial, generous-margins, refined, premium, magazine  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Premium brand decks, portfolio presentations, creative director pitches, and any context where the quality of the work should be signaled by the quality and restraint of the presentation design itself. Ideal for agencies, studios, and luxury brands.

---

## Visual Direction

Luxury magazine layouts where generous white space signals premium quality as forcefully as any other design choice. Every spread in Wallpaper*, Apartamento, or Cereal magazine has this quality — large margins, modest body type sizes, refined line spacing, photography given enormous room to breathe. The design communicates: we have the confidence to leave things out.

## Background & Texture

Pure white (#FFFFFF) or very warm ivory (#FDFAF5) — the specific white of high-quality coated paper stock. Ultra-subtle paper grain at near-invisible opacity. Wide margins (minimum 20% of slide width on each side) are the defining structural feature.

## Typography

Refined serif typeface for headlines (Cormorant Garamond, Playfair Display, or EB Garamond) alongside a clean sans-serif for body text and captions. The contrast between elegant serif display and clean sans body text creates sophisticated typographic tension. Generous leading (2× body size or more). Small body text — premium editorial design trusts the reader to lean in.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) — the dominant surface
- **Secondary:** Rich black (#111111) — primary text
- **Accent 1:** Warm gold (#C4922A) — thin rule lines, fine details
- **Accent 2:** Deep forest green (#234F2E) — alternative structural accent (or swap for deep navy #1A2F4A)
- **Neutral:** Warm light gray (#D8D3CB) — secondary text, captions, supporting elements

## Layout & Composition

Single large image taking 50–60% of the slide with vast white space alongside. Text positioned in a narrow column occupying perhaps 25% of the slide width, with proportionally enormous margins on either side. The image and text column are separated by generous gutters. Pull quotes may float into the margin zone at a slightly larger size. The layout feels spacious, considered, and unhurried.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Photography in clean, precise crops — no drop shadows, no frames. Single thin gold rule line (0.5pt) used as a refined section marker. Small bold italic category label ("PORTFOLIO", "PROJECT 01", "2024") in small caps above the headline. Occasional single simple geometric accent — a fine circle or a thin horizontal line. Page or section numbers in tiny type at the far margin corner.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Brand name in refined serif, large and centered with generous surrounding white space. One thin gold rule line. Volume and year in tiny small caps at bottom.
- **Content Slide:** Large image left, narrow text column right, gold rule line at top of text column, headline in serif, body in sans, caption below image.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered project phases with generous spacing between each, large numerals in light serif weight, brief phase titles, even briefer descriptions.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Single clean chart with refined color palette, generous space around it, supporting text positioned with editorial care.
- **Closing Slide:** Final statement in large refined serif, centered on white, gold rule line beneath, small contact or credit line at the bottom margin.

## Tone & Mood

Assured, quality-signaling, and refined. The tone communicates that the work shown is excellent enough to speak for itself without graphic embellishment. There is quiet pride and professional confidence in every generous margin. Audiences feel they are viewing something of genuine quality.

## Avoid

- Tight layouts that crowd elements together
- Saturated accent colors
- Decorative borders or patterns
- Large body text sizes (premium editorial trusts readers to read smaller type)
- Chunky geometric shapes
- Anything that would appear in a standard corporate presentation template

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in luxury white-space editorial style inspired by Wallpaper* and Cereal magazine. Use pure white background with very wide margins, a large clean photography placeholder taking the left 55% of the slide with no drop shadow, a narrow serif headline and clean sans-serif body text column in the right 25% of the slide separated by generous space, a single 0.5pt warm gold rule line above the text column, small caps category label above the headline, and generous line spacing throughout. The feel should be assured, premium, and quietly confident — white space as the primary design element communicating quality.
