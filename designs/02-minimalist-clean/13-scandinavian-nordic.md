# Design 13: Scandinavian / Nordic Design

**Category:** Minimalist & Clean  
**Style Tags:** Scandinavian, Nordic, hygge, functional-beauty, warm-minimal, natural  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Wellness brand presentations, product design showcases, lifestyle company pitches, and human-centered design workshops. Ideal for organizations that want to project warmth, trust, and thoughtful craftsmanship.

---

## Visual Direction

Danish and Swedish design principles applied to presentations — functional beauty, honest materials, warm neutrals, and human-centered clarity. The design feels handcrafted rather than mass-produced, thoughtful rather than efficient. Inspired by Muuto, HAY, IKEA's design language at its best, and the hygge concept of cozy, human warmth.

## Background & Texture

Warm off-white (#F6F1EB) with subtle natural paper or linen grain at very low opacity. Occasional birch wood or light stone texture as a section accent background. The background feels tactile — like quality card stock or natural cotton paper.

## Typography

Clean, humanist sans-serif as primary: Gill Sans, Proxima Nova, or Neue Montreal. Slightly rounded letterforms that feel warm rather than cold. Body text generously spaced with comfortable line-height. Occasional handwritten or semi-script accent typeface for warmth and human touch. Consistent, simple typographic hierarchy without aggressive size contrasts.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Warm charcoal (#3D3530) — main text, slightly warm rather than pure black
- **Secondary:** Natural off-white (#F6F1EB) — background
- **Accent 1:** Soft teal (#5C8F8A) — primary accent, structural elements
- **Accent 2:** Warm terracotta (#C4704A) — secondary accent, highlight moments
- **Neutral:** Birch beige (#C8B89A) — supporting elements, subtle dividers

## Layout & Composition

Generous margins and breathing room. Information presented in clean, well-spaced zones without crowding. Three-column grid used loosely — not mathematically strict but proportionally harmonious. Images are given space to breathe. A single large image alongside restrained body text is a favorite pattern. Whitespace is a feature, not a failure.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Simple line illustrations of natural objects (leaves, stones, wooden objects) as tasteful accents. Thin rounded-corner boxes for callouts. Subtle horizontal rule lines in terracotta or teal. Small geometric shapes (circle, simple diamond) as bullet point alternatives. Photography in warm, naturally lit style — never clinical or artificial.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Large, friendly humanist sans-serif headline, generous spacing, a simple natural illustration accent, warm terracotta or teal rule line below the title.
- **Content Slide:** Three-zone layout — one image taking 40%, two content blocks beside it with generous margins and clean typography.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Soft numbered circles in teal, each connected by a thin rule, short descriptive labels in warm charcoal, flowing left to right with natural pacing.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Clean bar or line chart in teal and terracotta, simplified to essential data only, warm charcoal labels, summary statistic highlighted simply.
- **Closing Slide:** Single warm headline, a natural illustration accent, and a simple contact or next-step line in small type.

## Tone & Mood

Warm, trustworthy, and thoughtfully human. The tone communicates care — for the audience, for the craft, for the quality of the ideas. There is quiet confidence here without aggression. Audiences feel welcomed and respected.

## Avoid

- Cold blue-gray corporate palettes
- Hard-edged geometric shapes
- Aggressive type scales or urgent visual energy
- Stock photography (especially overly polished lifestyle shots)
- Multiple competing accent colors
- Digital UI aesthetics or tech-forward visual language

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in Scandinavian / Nordic style inspired by Danish design principles and hygge warmth. Use a natural off-white background with subtle linen grain texture, warm charcoal as primary text color, clean humanist sans-serif typography with generous line spacing, soft teal and warm terracotta as accent colors, a large naturally-lit photography placeholder beside restrained body text with generous margins, simple thin rule line dividers, and a small natural illustration accent. The feel should be warm, trusted, and humanly crafted — functional beauty with no unnecessary elements.
