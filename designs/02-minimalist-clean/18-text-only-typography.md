# Design 18: Text-Only Typographic Design

**Category:** Minimalist & Clean  
**Style Tags:** typographic, text-only, no-image, Swiss, bold, idea-forward  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** TED-style intellectual talks, philosophical or argumentative presentations, and contexts where the idea itself — expressed in carefully chosen words — is the entire content. Ideal for confident communicators who trust language as their primary medium.

---

## Visual Direction

No images, no icons, no illustrations. Pure typographic composition where varying weights, sizes, spacing, and alignment create all visual hierarchy and visual interest. The slide is a poem, an argument, a manifesto — expressed entirely through the arrangement of type. Inspired by Swiss poster design, typographic experiment, and editorial minimalism.

## Background & Texture

Pure white (#FFFFFF) — no competition with the typography. Occasionally a full-bleed solid color background (deep black, vivid single color) for high-drama contrast slides. No texture, no pattern, no atmospheric effect. The background is a stage for the words.

## Typography

The only tool and therefore used with extreme sophistication. A single typeface family in its full weight range — from hairline to ultra-black. Scale range spans the full slide: from enormous display type that fills the entire width to tiny caption text barely legible at the bottom. Position, alignment, size, weight, spacing, and color (limited to black and one accent) carry all visual meaning.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Pure black (#000000) — primary typographic presence
- **Secondary:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) — background
- **Accent 1:** Single vivid color (chosen per presentation) — for one critical word or phrase per slide maximum
- **Accent 2:** (none — the single accent IS the accent)
- **Neutral:** Mid gray (#888888) — secondary, supporting, or archival text elements

## Layout & Composition

Grid-defying composition where type itself creates the grid. Oversized letters in one zone create visual weight that smaller text elsewhere must balance. Text may overlap at slight opacity for layered meaning. Justified blocks versus ragged-right paragraphs create compositional zones. A single word scaled to fill the full width of the slide creates a horizon line that structures everything else.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Typography IS the graphic element. Occasional thin rule line (0.5–1pt) as a structural separator. A single solid geometric shape (rectangle, circle) in the accent color — but only as a background behind critical text, never as a floating decoration. No icons, no illustrations, no photography.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Presentation title in massive weight-contrasted type, sub-headline in tiny light weight far below with significant space, no other elements.
- **Content Slide:** One key idea in large display type, supporting explanation in smaller medium weight below, a single short counter-point or question in italic near the edge.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered sequence with the numbers themselves at enormous scale, step descriptions in normal body text beside each number.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** The statistic IS the slide — one number at maximum scale, with the context sentence in small type below.
- **Closing Slide:** Single phrase, single word, or single punctuation mark. The typographic choice itself is the final message.

## Tone & Mood

Confident, intellectual, and uncompromising. The presenter using this style is saying: the words are enough. There is boldness in rejecting the visual crutch of images and trusting language and typography to do all the work. Audiences feel the weight of carefully chosen words.

## Avoid

- Any images, icons, or illustrations of any kind
- Multiple typeface families
- Decorative elements
- Busy or cluttered text arrangements (chaos is not this style — precision is)
- Consistent middle-weight type across all text (the entire hierarchy rests on weight and size contrast)
- Multiple accent colors

## Example Prompt

> Create a presentation slide using pure typographic design with no images, icons, or illustrations. Use a single typeface family (Helvetica Neue, Futura, or similar geometric sans) across its full weight range from hairline to ultra-black, on a pure white background. Scale the primary headline to fill the full width of the slide in ultra-black weight, set supporting text in small light weight far below with generous space between, and highlight one critical word using a single vivid accent color. Create all visual hierarchy through type size, weight, position, and spacing alone. The feel should be bold, intellectual, and uncompromising — typographic design as an argument in itself.
