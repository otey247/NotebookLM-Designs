# Design 93: Startup Founder Pitch

**Category:** Branding & Identity  
**Style Tags:** startup, pitch-deck, founder, Y-Combinator, investor, narrative  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Seed-stage and Series A investor pitches, Y Combinator demo day presentations, accelerator program pitches, and founder-led narrative presentations to early-stage investors. Ideal for startup founders and early-stage companies making their first major investor impression.

---

## Visual Direction

Y Combinator demo day presentation aesthetics, Airbnb pitch deck simplicity, Sequoia pitch framework — a single-minded narrative arc: problem → solution → market → product → traction → team → ask. Each slide carries one idea. The founder's authentic voice drives the content. Simplicity is courage — removing anything that doesn't directly advance the narrative.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) with one strong accent color that represents the startup's brand. No texture — startup pitches are digital-first and pattern-free. The brand color is the personality; everything else is structural.

## Typography

Clean modern sans-serif — Inter, Nunito, or the startup's brand font. Bold for slide headlines (the "one thing per slide" headline). Regular for body bullets. Large numbers for traction metrics. The typographic system should look like it was designed, not assembled from defaults.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Startup brand color (define per company — example: vivid teal #0EA5E9)
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — primary surface
- **Accent 1:** Near-black (#1A1A1A) — headlines, bold text
- **Accent 2:** Light brand tint (brand color at 10% opacity) — subtle highlight zones
- **Neutral:** Cool gray (#6B7280) — secondary text, supporting information

## Layout & Composition

Sequoia pitch structure slides: one thought, one visual, one proof point per slide. Clean and generous whitespace. The headline IS the slide — a complete sentence that communicates the insight. Key metrics as large-scale numbers. Product screenshot as the product slide's primary element. Team slide with photographs. Traction chart as the evidence slide.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Problem slide: a painful statistic or real customer quote. Solution slide: product screenshot or simple "before/after" illustration. Market size: TAM/SAM/SOM in concentric circles or bar chart. Traction: simple growth curve chart with key milestones. Team slide: headshots in circles, roles, and relevant credentials. Funding slide: round size, use of funds.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Company name, tagline in one sentence ("The Airbnb for X" or authentic one-liner), logo, and presenter's name.
- **Content Slide (Problem):** "We solve [specific problem] for [specific customer]" — one painful statistic, one customer voice quote.
- **Process/Flow Slide (Solution):** Product screenshot with 3 key features labeled, or "Before / After" comparison with the key metric improvement.
- **Data/Chart Slide (Traction):** Revenue or usage growth chart with 12–18 months of data, milestone annotations, hockey-stick growth labeled.
- **Closing Slide (Ask):** "$X [at what valuation]" — specific ask, use of funds in three bullets, investors already in, contact information.

## Tone & Mood

Founder-authentic, confident, and focused. The tone communicates: we understand this problem deeply, we've built the right solution, and we have evidence it's working. There is founder conviction without arrogance. Investors feel they are hearing from someone who knows this space better than anyone.

## Avoid

- More than 12–15 slides (less is more in a pitch)
- Technical complexity (investors invest in problems and teams, not features)
- Overly detailed financial models before they're asked for
- Complicated slide layouts (one thought per slide)
- Weak or hedged problem statements
- Underestimating the market or being dishonest about traction

## Example Prompt

> Design a startup founder pitch deck slide in Y Combinator demo day style. Use a clean white background with the startup's vivid teal brand color (#0EA5E9) as the accent, one bold headline at the top stating the slide's single insight ("We've grown 10× in 12 months"), a large traction growth chart in the center showing 18 months of revenue data with month-over-month growth highlighted, three key milestone annotations on the chart in bold teal dots with labels, a simple bold statistic in large type beside the chart ($X ARR), company logo small in the top left corner. The feel should be founder-authentic, evidence-confident, and narratively compelling — startup pitch deck aesthetics at its clearest.
