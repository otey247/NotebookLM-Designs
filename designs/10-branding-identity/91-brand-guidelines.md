# Design 91: Brand Guidelines Presentation

**Category:** Branding & Identity  
**Style Tags:** brand-guidelines, style-guide, brand-identity, systematic, Pentagram, logo  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Brand launch presentations, style guide documentation, brand training sessions, rebranding reveals, and internal brand alignment presentations. Ideal for brand managers, identity designers, and marketing leaders communicating a brand system to internal or external stakeholders.

---

## Visual Direction

Pentagram, Wolff Olins, and Interbrand brand guidelines aesthetics — the specific visual authority of a brand system document that shows the rules by which a brand is built. Logo presentation at multiple scales and orientations, color palette with precise specifications, typography system with usage hierarchy, brand asset grid, and do/don't misuse examples.

## Background & Texture

The brand's own primary background color — this document demonstrates the brand rather than describing it. Every design choice demonstrates the brand in action. If the brand background is dark, this document is dark. If it is white, it is white. The guidelines ARE the brand application.

## Typography

The brand's own typography system in use — demonstrating the typographic rules as they apply. Display heading in the brand's display face. Body text in the brand's text face. System-level information (color codes, font names) in a neutral monospace as specifications.

## Color Palette

Brand-defined palette (specific values will vary per brand):
- **Primary brand color** — the brand's main color
- **Secondary brand color** — the supporting palette color
- **Text color** — the brand's standard text treatment
- **Background color** — the brand's standard background
- **Accent color** — the brand's highlight/action color

*The example uses: Electric blue (#0066CC), White (#FFFFFF), Near-black (#1A1A1A), Light gray (#F5F5F5), Accent orange (#FF6600)*

## Layout & Composition

Brand guidelines page format: large white space areas for brand elements to breathe. Logo presented at full scale, then at reduced scales. Color palette swatches in a row with Hex, RGB, and CMYK values beneath each. Typography specimens showing each typeface at key display sizes. Photography style examples. Grid and layout system illustration.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Logo presentations at different sizes and on different backgrounds (white, primary color, black). Color swatch grid with specification values. Typography specimen blocks showing font name, size, weight, and sample text. "Do/Don't" example pairs. Brand photography style guide examples. Icon set preview grid. White space and grid system illustration. Logo clear space diagram.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Brand name and tagline, the brand symbol at large scale, and "Brand Identity Guidelines" as the document title on the brand's primary background.
- **Content Slide:** Logo presentation page — logo at master scale, variations (horizontal, stacked, symbol-only), minimum size, clear space requirements.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Brand rollout plan — where the brand appears, in what sequence, with what priority, as a phased deployment timeline.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Color palette page — all color swatches with hex, RGB, CMYK, Pantone values, primary/secondary/neutral designation.
- **Closing Slide:** "Brand is what people experience" — brand values stated on the primary brand color background with the logo.

## Tone & Mood

Systematically authoritative, brand-confident, and design-precise. The tone communicates: this brand has been thoroughly thought through, every rule is deliberate, and every application tells the same consistent story. Brand managers feel equipped with what they need; partners feel the confidence of working with a well-defined identity.

## Avoid

- Using colors, fonts, or design elements outside the brand system
- Informal or casual layout treatment
- Inconsistency between the guidelines' stated rules and its own design
- Missing technical specifications (hex codes, font names)
- Overcrowded pages that don't demonstrate the brand's breathing room
- Inconsistent logo scaling or positioning

## Example Prompt

> Design a brand guidelines presentation slide in professional identity design style inspired by Pentagram and Wolff Olins brand document aesthetics. Use the brand's primary electric blue (#0066CC) as the background, the brand logo at large scale centered in the upper zone in white, the brand's typography system displayed below (display headline in the brand font, body text in the secondary font), a row of color swatches along the bottom third showing the complete color palette with hex values beneath each swatch, the slide structured on a generous grid with consistent margins. The feel should be systematically authoritative, brand-precise, and design-confident — a brand guidelines document as demonstration of the brand itself.
