# Design 98: Travel & Destination Brand

**Category:** Branding & Identity  
**Style Tags:** travel, destination, vintage-poster, tourism, wanderlust, geography  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Tourism board presentations, travel brand pitches, destination marketing materials, hospitality industry content, and any presentation where inspiring wanderlust and communicating the unique character of a place is the goal. Ideal for travel brands, tourism boards, and hospitality marketers.

---

## Visual Direction

WPA National Parks poster aesthetics, 1930s Art Deco travel poster tradition (Pan Am, Orient Express), contemporary destination photography editorial — the bold graphic simplifications of mid-century travel posters merged with modern destination photography, creating the "wanderlust aesthetic" that defines contemporary travel brand communication.

## Background & Texture

Destination-specific: deep sky blue (#0A3A6A) and land gold (#C4892A) for the WPA poster aesthetic, or full-bleed destination photography for the contemporary editorial approach. Subtle linen or poster texture for the vintage approach.

## Typography

WPA poster-style all-caps block lettering for headlines — bold, clear, readable. Vintage serif for destination name. Clean modern sans for contemporary travel brands. Geographic details in small condensed type (elevation, coordinates, founding date). The typography should feel like it belongs to the destination's visual identity.

## Color Palette

- **WPA Vintage:**
  - **Primary:** Destination blue (#0A3A6A) — sky, water
  - **Secondary:** Land gold (#C4892A) — terrain, warmth
  - **Accent 1:** Forest green (#2D6A2A) — forests, nature
  - **Accent 2:** Terracotta (#C4602A) — earth, desert
  - **Neutral:** Cream (#F5EDD8) — typography, light zone

- **Contemporary:**
  - **Primary:** Destination photography — the world itself
  - **Secondary:** White (#FFFFFF) — type
  - **Accent:** Place-specific brand color

## Layout & Composition

WPA poster format: bold simplified landscape in the upper 60%, destination name at large scale, subtext at the bottom. Contemporary format: full-bleed photography with white or light type overlay. Both use the "window to somewhere" composition — the slide feels like looking through a frame at a beautiful place.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Simplified landscape illustration in WPA style (bold color masses, no detail, maximum graphic impact). Destination name in vintage display lettering. Geographic coordinate typography. National Park ranger shield or destination badge. Vintage airline/railroad ticket format information. Journey distance or travel time. Destination-specific iconic element (Eiffel Tower silhouette, palm tree, mountain peak).

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** WPA-style destination poster or full-bleed photography, destination name in bold display type, "Presented by [Organization]" in small type below.
- **Content Slide:** Destination feature — photograph + compelling fact + authentic local detail, editorial travel magazine layout.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Journey itinerary as a travel route map, day-by-day sequence, destination badge at each stop.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Tourism statistics with destination warmth — visitor numbers, economic impact, with landscape photography backdrop.
- **Closing Slide:** Single stunning destination image, bold invitation headline ("Your next destination"), contact and booking information.

## Tone & Mood

Wanderlust-inspiring, authentically specific to place, and invitingly warm. The tone communicates: this place is genuinely extraordinary, and you belong here experiencing it. There is no generic "travel" — every element is specific to the unique character of this destination. Audiences feel the specific desire to be there.

## Avoid

- Generic stock photography without destination specificity
- Tourist cliché without authentic perspective
- WPA aesthetic without understanding of the tradition
- Multiple destination visual identities on one slide
- Information density that kills the wanderlust
- Corporate visual language

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in WPA National Parks poster style for a destination travel brand. Use a simplified bold graphic illustration of a mountain and lake landscape in the WPA palette (deep sky blue, forest green, golden land tones, terracotta), the destination name in vintage all-caps block lettering at large scale in the lower third in cream on a dark blue band, "NATIONAL PARK" or equivalent designation below the name in condensed spaced caps, elevation and coordinates in very small type as authentic geographic details, and the visual simplification style of 1930s–40s government poster art (bold color masses, no photographic detail). The feel should be bold, graphically iconic, and wanderlust-inspiring — WPA travel poster aesthetics.
