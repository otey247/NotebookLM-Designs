# Design 96: Food & Restaurant Brand

**Category:** Branding & Identity  
**Style Tags:** food, restaurant, culinary, farm-to-table, menu-design, appetite  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Restaurant pitch presentations, food brand launches, culinary program marketing materials, food publication content, and any context where making food — and the culture around food — feel irresistibly appealing. Ideal for food entrepreneurs, chefs, and culinary brand managers.

---

## Visual Direction

Ottolenghi cookbook aesthetics, Bon Appétit magazine food editorial, fine dining menu typography, and farm-to-table brand visual language — food photography with natural light on linen surfaces, handwritten chalk menu board type, botanical illustration accents, warm earth tones suggesting freshness and provenance, and the specific sensory excitement of great food communication.

## Background & Texture

Warm natural surfaces — linen white (#F8F3EC), aged wood grain (#8B6347), slate gray (#4A5568), or warm cream chalkboard (#1A1A14). The surface texture IS part of the food communication — linen and wood suggest craft and quality. Food photography works on these surfaces.

## Typography

Two complementary typefaces: a script or handwritten-style for headlines and key menu items (Pacifico, Baskervilles, Dancing Script) paired with a clean text serif (Garamond, Freight Text) for body and descriptions. Menu-style typography: item name on the left, price on the right, description in italic smaller type below. Chalk lettering style as an optional board-inspired alternative.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Warm cream linen (#F8F3EC) — the natural food surface
- **Secondary:** Earth deep brown (#4A2A0A) — the warm near-black for type and accents
- **Accent 1:** Herb green (#2D6A2A) — fresh herbs, farm freshness, botanical
- **Accent 2:** Warm terracotta (#C4602A) — warmth, spice, artisan quality
- **Neutral:** Warm gold (#C4892A) — accent line, price typography, premium accent

## Layout & Composition

Food photography as the primary visual element — ingredients or finished dishes photographed with natural light on textured surfaces. Typography layered over or alongside the photography. Menu-format content with deliberate typographic spacing. Recipe or ingredient list as a design element. Botanical line illustration as decorative accent. Generous white space that makes the food breathe.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Botanical line illustrations of herbs, vegetables, and spices. Handwritten-style annotations on photographs ("fresh from the garden", ingredient labels). Menu format text with right-aligned prices and italic descriptions. Circular emblem or stamp graphic for brand marks. Watercolor food illustration elements. Decorative divider lines in the style of a printed menu. Chalkboard lettering zones.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Food photography background (hero dish or ingredient arrangement), restaurant name in script display type, tagline in small italic serif, "Est. [year]" as a founding credential.
- **Content Slide:** Menu section format — section header in script, three or four items with descriptions in text serif, prices in warm gold, botanical illustration accent.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Farm-to-table journey — ingredient source → kitchen process → plate presentation, each stage with a photograph.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Sales or nutrition data presented with food brand warmth — statistics in clean type on a warm textured background with botanical accents.
- **Closing Slide:** One beautiful food photograph taking the full slide, restaurant name and tagline in the natural light zone of the image.

## Tone & Mood

Sensory-warm, authentically crafted, and genuinely appetizing. The tone communicates: this food was made with care and will taste of quality. There is real pleasure in the visual language of good food — natural textures, warm colors, and the specific anticipation that well-photographed food creates. Audiences feel hungry (in the best way).

## Avoid

- Cool or clinical color palettes
- Photographs without natural light warmth
- Corporate sans-serif typography
- Overly ornate decoration that competes with the food
- Artificial-looking food photography
- Missing the specific sensory warmth that makes food content compelling

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in farm-to-table restaurant brand style. Use a warm linen background (#F8F3EC) with subtle fabric texture, a hero dish food photography placeholder in the left 60% of the slide with natural light and a linen surface, the restaurant name in an elegant script display typeface in warm earth brown on the right, the tagline in small italic Garamond below, botanical herb illustration accents (rosemary, thyme branches) in the lower corners in fine line style, a warm gold thin rule line separating title elements, and "EST. 2018" in small spaced all-caps. The feel should be sensory-warm, naturally crafted, and irresistibly appetizing — artisan food brand aesthetics applied to presentation design.
