# Design 92: Logo Design Showcase

**Category:** Branding & Identity  
**Style Tags:** logo, identity, symbol-mark, logotype, wordmark, brand-evolution  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Logo reveal presentations, rebrand announcement decks, logo design pitches, identity design agency presentations, and any context presenting a visual identity at its most reduced and powerful. Ideal for brand designers and marketing leaders.

---

## Visual Direction

Behance brand identity case study aesthetics — clean dark or white backgrounds that let the logo breathe, progressive reveal of logo construction, color version presentations, scaled application examples, and the specific pride of showing a well-designed identity mark at the moment of its unveiling.

## Background & Texture

Dramatic dark navy (#0A0A2E) or pure black (#000000) for the logo reveal moment — dark backgrounds make logos pop with maximum presence. Alternatively, pure white (#FFFFFF) for clean daytime presentation contexts. The background serves the logo — no competition.

## Typography

Clean, neutral sans-serif for all supporting presentation text — the logo is the typography hero. System and annotation text in a geometric sans at restrained scale. The only prominent typography is the logotype itself being presented.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Dark navy (#0A0A2E) — dramatic reveal background
- **Secondary:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) — logo against dark backgrounds
- **Accent 1:** Logo primary color — whatever the identity primary is
- **Accent 2:** Logo secondary color — the brand's secondary identity color
- **Neutral:** Mid gray (#6B7280) — supporting labels and specifications

## Layout & Composition

Single logo at maximum presence — the logo takes 40–60% of the slide, centered, with breathing room on all sides. Supporting context is minimal and positioned well outside the logo's clear space. The grid is defined by the logo's clear space rules.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Logo construction grid overlay (for "making of" slides). Clear space demonstration lines (showing the minimum white space required). Logo on different background colors (white logo on dark, dark logo on light, color logo). Scale demonstration (from large to small to minimum readable size). Animation path arrows for identity motion directions.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Logo in maximum darkness reveal — dark background, logo in white or primary color at 50% of the slide centered, company name in small clean type below.
- **Content Slide:** Logo construction slide — showing the geometric or conceptual system behind the mark, with grid lines and proportions.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Brand evolution timeline — previous logos to new, showing the design evolution with dates.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Logo color specifications — the logo on multiple backgrounds with hex/CMYK/Pantone values at each.
- **Closing Slide:** Primary logo in its primary color context at full scale — the definitive statement of the identity.

## Tone & Mood

Identity-proud, carefully unveiled, and brand-confident. The tone communicates: this mark has been designed with precision for a specific purpose, and that purpose is visible in every proportion and decision. A great logo presentation makes the audience feel they are seeing something inevitable — this is exactly what this organization should look like.

## Avoid

- Cluttered backgrounds that compete with the logo
- Multiple logos on one slide (unless specifically comparing or showing variations)
- Text that interrupts the logo's visual field
- Showing the logo before the conceptual context is established
- Non-branded supporting typography
- Skewing, distorting, or outlining the logo in ways that violate brand rules

## Example Prompt

> Design a logo design showcase presentation slide. Use a deep dark navy background (#0A0A2E), the brand logo placeholder (a simple geometric symbol + wordmark) in white at 50% of the slide surface centered with generous clear space on all sides, below the logo: the logo's name in small clean neutral sans, a small row of color version previews at the bottom (logo on white, logo in primary color, logo inverted), and a brief construction note showing the golden ratio or geometric basis of the symbol. The feel should be dramatically revealing, identity-proud, and brand-confident — a logo design showcase at the moment of unveiling.
