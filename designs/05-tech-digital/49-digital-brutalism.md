# Design 49: Digital Brutalism

**Category:** Tech & Digital  
**Style Tags:** digital-brutalism, anti-design, post-ironic, system-aesthetic, web-brutalism, intentional  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** Design conferences, cultural commentary talks, experimental creative pitches, and presentations about design history or the concept of aesthetic value. Ideal for design critics, experimental studios, and culture-literate creative audiences.

---

## Visual Direction

Web brutalism applied to slide design — system fonts used with full awareness of their baggage, raw HTML-box aesthetics, visible borders around everything, tables as both functional and decorative design elements, no border radius anywhere, anti-beauty as a sophisticated aesthetic position. The goal is not to be accidentally ugly but to be deliberately, meaningfully, knowingly ugly.

## Background & Texture

Pure white (#FFFFFF) — the default HTML page background. This is the starting point the brutalist keeps because it refuses decoration. No color, no gradient, no texture. The whiteness is aggressive in its refusal.

## Typography

System fonts used with full, deliberate awareness: Arial for headings (acknowledging its corporate banality), Times New Roman for body (its institutional weight), Courier New for technical elements (its typewriter pretension). Comic Sans appears exactly once (and the audience must decide if it's a joke). Bold type at system default sizes. Underlined text that implies hyperlinks.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) — the refusal
- **Secondary:** Pure black (#000000) — system-default text
- **Accent 1:** Hyperlink blue (#0000EE) — the color of trust, worn smooth by overuse
- **Accent 2:** Visited purple (#551A8B) — the click that already happened
- **Neutral:** Default border gray (#808080) — every HTML element's own default

## Layout & Composition

HTML table structure as composition system — visible 1px black borders around all content cells. Elements sized by their content, not by designer intent. Text bleeds to the edge of its container. Images at their native pixel dimensions (potentially awkward). The layout has the quality of something rendered by a browser in 1996 without a stylesheet.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Horizontal rule lines (the classic HR element). Visible table cell borders. Animated GIF placeholders (small spinning icons, under-construction banners). Low-resolution favicon-style images at large scale. Horizontal scrollbar metaphor (content that exceeds its container). Bullet points using the default browser filled circles. Bold and italic used for emphasis without visual design logic.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Arial title in large bold, centered on white, black border around the text block, underlined subtitle as a hyperlink, "Welcome to [Presentation Name]" as the H1 equivalent.
- **Content Slide:** HTML table with visible borders, content in cells, different cell background colors (#CCCCCC for one, white for another), mixed type sizes.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered list in Times New Roman, no other visual treatment, 1–5 items. The list is the design.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Tables. Just tables. Border-collapse: separate. 1px solid black borders. Row headers in bold.
- **Closing Slide:** "THE END" in Times New Roman at 400% scale on pure white. Underlined. Hyperlink blue. That's it.

## Tone & Mood

Intellectually provocative, post-ironic, and sophisticated in its refusal of sophistication. The tone communicates awareness of every design convention it is violating and the philosophical position that convention-violation can be as meaningful as convention-following. Design-literate audiences recognize the argument being made.

## Avoid

- Accidental ugliness (this must be deliberate and controlled)
- Inconsistency within the system (even anti-design has its own consistency)
- Elements that signal incompetence rather than philosophy
- Colorful accents beyond the defined HTML-default palette
- Any visual element that suggests trying to look attractive
- Missing the point: this is POST-ironic, not just ironic — there is genuine design thinking here

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in digital brutalism style using pure web-default aesthetics. Use pure white background, Arial for headlines in large bold, Times New Roman for body text, 1px solid black borders around all content containers (table-style with visible cell borders), hyperlink blue (#0000EE) for one underlined element, default bullet point circles for a list, a horizontal rule as a section divider, Comic Sans for exactly one element (deliberately placed with full awareness), and a low-resolution image at awkward native size. The feel should be deliberate, knowing, and intellectually provocative — anti-design as a sophisticated philosophical position expressed through system-default aesthetics.
