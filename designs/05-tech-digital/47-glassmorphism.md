# Design 47: Glassmorphism

**Category:** Tech & Digital  
**Style Tags:** glassmorphism, iOS, frosted-glass, translucent, depth, premium-digital  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Product demos, SaaS platform presentations, mobile app pitches, design system showcases, and technology-forward presentations for premium consumer products. Ideal for product managers, app designers, and technology brand teams.

---

## Visual Direction

iOS and macOS frosted-glass panels, translucent overlapping cards with bokeh blur backgrounds, subtle white borders at reduced opacity, layered depth without hard shadows, and the characteristic contemporary premium-digital aesthetic. The design communicates: this experience has been carefully designed at every layer.

## Background & Texture

Vivid gradient backgrounds (purple to deep blue, warm sunset orange to coral, or teal to cyan) provide the color substrate that makes glass panels readable. The background should be saturated and beautiful because it shows through the glass. The background IS as important as the glass elements in front of it.

## Typography

Clean, modern sans-serif (SF Pro, Inter, or equivalent) in white or very light tints on glass surfaces. Weight contrast for hierarchy (Bold headline, Regular body) on glass panels. The typography floats within the glass, appearing to exist at a different depth layer from the background.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Gradient purple-to-blue (#7B2FBE to #2563EB) — background gradient
- **Secondary:** Glass white (#FFFFFF at 10–20% opacity) — frosted glass panel surface
- **Accent 1:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) — primary text on glass, borders at 30% opacity
- **Accent 2:** Vivid accent color (#0EA5E9 or #8B5CF6) — one vivid element per glass panel
- **Neutral:** White at 60–80% opacity — secondary text on glass panels

## Layout & Composition

Layered depth composition: vivid gradient background → frosted glass panels floating at different elevations → typography and icons within glass panels → small sharp accent elements on top. Glass panels can overlap — the translucency makes overlap readable. Each panel floats independently with a thin white border and subtle inner shadow. Bokeh blur circles in the background add depth.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Frosted glass panel components: `backdrop-filter: blur(20px)` aesthetic with 15% white fill and 30% white border. Bokeh blur circles (soft focus gradient spheres) in the background at various scales. Subtle inner shadow on glass panels. Thin white border (1px at 30% opacity) on all glass elements. Blur-through effect showing background gradient through glass content.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Vivid gradient full-bleed background, large frosted glass card centered with presentation title in bold white, small frosted pill chips as category labels, bokeh spheres in background.
- **Content Slide:** Three floating glass panels in a grid, each with icon, label, and body text in white, vivid accent color for icon backgrounds, bokeh blur spheres in gradient background.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Sequential frosted glass nodes connected by glowing lines, each node floating at slightly different depths, gradient progression in background.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Primary chart on frosted glass panel, key metric as a large white number with small glass label cards for supporting data, all floating over gradient.
- **Closing Slide:** Single large frosted glass panel with CTA, gradient background, bokeh accents.

## Tone & Mood

Ethereal, contemporary, and premium-digital. The tone communicates that this product or experience exists in a visually refined technological space — designed with care for aesthetic depth as well as functional clarity. Audiences feel the quality of the visual experience.

## Avoid

- Opaque backgrounds (the gradient must be visible through the glass)
- Dark mode (glassmorphism is a light/colorful aesthetic)
- Heavy shadows or harsh edges on glass panels
- Too many glass layers making the composition unreadable
- Non-gradient backgrounds (flat colors don't create the glass effect)
- Typography that doesn't have sufficient contrast on glass surfaces

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in glassmorphism style. Use a vivid full-bleed gradient background (deep purple to cobalt blue), floating frosted glass panels at 15% white opacity with 1px white border at 30% opacity and backdrop blur effect, bokeh gradient sphere elements at various scales in the background, bold white typography on the glass surfaces, a three-glass-panel grid with vivid accent color (#0EA5E9) icon backgrounds within each panel, translucent pill-shaped chip labels, and subtle inner shadow on all glass panels. The feel should be ethereal, depth-layered, and premium-digital — iOS/macOS frosted glass aesthetic applied to presentation design.
