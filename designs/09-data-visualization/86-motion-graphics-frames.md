# Design 86: Motion Graphics & Animation Frames

**Category:** Data & Visualization  
**Style Tags:** motion-graphics, animation, frame-by-frame, After-Effects, kinetic, progressive  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** Video presentation content, animated explainer planning, motion design briefings, and static presentations that simulate animation through progressive reveal techniques. Ideal for motion designers, video producers, and creative directors.

---

## Visual Direction

After Effects and Cinema 4D motion graphics production aesthetics — keyframe storyboards, easing curves, timing charts, progressive element reveal, and the vocabulary of animated presentation design. The static slide communicates the intended motion through visual cues: path arrows, animation timing indicators, and progressive element states.

## Background & Texture

Clean dark charcoal (#1E1E24) — the professional motion graphics work environment. Alternatively: the final animated composition background (whatever the motion piece uses). Animation guides and frame numbers are overlay elements.

## Typography

Clean, modern geometric sans-serif — the motion graphics tradition. Headlines in a bold geometric sans (Montserrat, Futura), animated elements in the same family. Timing notation in monospace (00:00:12, @24fps). Easing curve labels in small technical type.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Deep charcoal (#1E1E24) — composition background
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — foreground elements
- **Accent 1:** Motion blue (#4F9CF9) — path arrows, animation guides
- **Accent 2:** Keyframe orange (#FFB86C) — keyframe markers, timing indicators
- **Neutral:** Mid gray (#6A7A8A) — ghost state elements, inactive frames

## Layout & Composition

Composition frame with motion design element guides: path arrows showing motion trajectories, opacity progression indicators, scale change annotations, rotation markers. Multiple states of an animated element shown at different time codes. Keyframe diamonds at start/end points. Easing curve visualization below or beside the composition.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Path arrows showing animation trajectories. Keyframe diamond markers. Timing bar (timeline strip) at the bottom. Easing curve graphic (ease-in, ease-out, spring). Ghost state elements in reduced opacity showing start/end positions. Frame counter overlay. Composition boundary frame. Layer stack indicator. AE-style property indicators (Position, Scale, Rotation, Opacity).

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Final composition view — the hero animation frame at its most visually complete state, title in animated display style.
- **Content Slide:** Key animation frame with motion path arrows showing trajectories, position markers at start/end, timing annotations.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Storyboard of animation keyframes — 4–6 frames showing the animation progression with time codes beneath each.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Animated chart progression — three states of a chart showing data entering the composition, with progress indicators.
- **Closing Slide:** Final composition still — the completed animation frame — with timing and production credits.

## Tone & Mood

Motion-aware, technically precise, and dynamically visual. The tone communicates: this content exists in time, and the design accounts for time as a dimension. There is technical sophistication in the motion design vocabulary that signals professional production quality. Audiences see the design intent before the final animation is produced.

## Avoid

- Static layout that doesn't communicate the motion intent
- Missing timing or trajectory information
- Decoration that simulates motion without planning actual motion
- Over-complicated motion paths
- Missing ghost states for before/after comparisons
- Motion annotations that are unclear about direction or sequence

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in motion graphics and animation frame style. Use a deep charcoal composition background, the hero animated element (a data chart or typographic element) shown in its keyframe state, motion path arrows showing its entry trajectory in blue with directional arrowheads, ghost state element at 30% opacity showing the start position, keyframe diamond markers at the start and end points, a timing bar at the bottom showing the 3-second animation duration with keyframe markers, an easing curve visualization (ease-in-out) beside the path, and frame counter overlay (00:00:12 @ 24fps). The feel should be motion-design-precise, technically communicative, and animation-vocabulary authentic.
