# Design 88: Environmental & Climate Data

**Category:** Data & Visualization  
**Style Tags:** environmental, climate, IPCC, sustainability, earth-science, urgency  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Climate science communications, environmental program presentations, sustainability reporting, ESG performance briefings, and any data presentation where the environmental stakes must be communicated with both scientific precision and public clarity. Ideal for climate scientists, sustainability professionals, and environmental advocates.

---

## Visual Direction

IPCC Assessment Report figure standards, NASA Earth Observatory visualization aesthetics, and the visual vocabulary of climate science communication — temperature anomaly heatmaps, cumulative emissions area charts, sea level rise projections with uncertainty ranges, and the specific visual weight of data that represents irreversible change.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) for scientific authority, or deep space view of Earth (#0A0A1A with blue-green earth) for the global perspective. The IPCC uses white backgrounds for their authoritative reports — but the data itself carries the atmospheric weight.

## Typography

Clean, authoritative sans-serif following scientific communication standards — matches IPCC AR6 report aesthetic (Calibri, Gill Sans, or equivalent). Bold for key finding statements. Standard scientific notation for temperature (°C), CO₂ (ppm), sea level (mm), and uncertainty ranges (±). Clear axis labels with units. "Panel A, Panel B" labeling system.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** IPCC red (#C8232C) — temperature anomaly high values, emissions, urgency
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — background
- **Accent 1:** IPCC blue (#3056A0) — cooling anomalies, water, ice, lower bound
- **Accent 2:** IPCC amber (#F5A500) — moderate values, intermediate scenarios
- **Neutral:** Earth gray (#7A7A7A) — uncertainty shading, historical reference periods

## Layout & Composition

Multi-panel scientific figure layout. Time series charts with long baselines (1850–present or 1950–2100). Uncertainty range shading behind projection lines. Scenario comparison with multiple colored lines (SSP1-1.9 through SSP5-8.5). Global map views for spatial data. Legend with full scenario name explanation. Reference period baseline annotations.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Temperature anomaly color scale (blue-white-red). Uncertainty range shading (lighter color, usually ±1σ or 5–95th percentile). Scenario lines in distinct colors with scenario labels. "Observed" line in solid black. Projection period shift indicator. Paris Agreement temperature target lines (1.5°C, 2°C horizontal references). Climate tipping point marker. Confidence interval notation. "Very likely" / "Likely" uncertainty qualifier language.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** IPCC-style report cover with the key finding as the headline, global temperature anomaly map, year and scenario.
- **Content Slide:** Multi-panel figure with observed and projected time series, uncertainty ranges, scenario comparison, key finding callout box.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Carbon budget diagram — total budget, current trajectory, required change — as a flow visualization.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Regional impact map or time series, uncertainty range, comparison to scenarios, summary statistics.
- **Closing Slide:** Mitigation pathways — what would need to happen in each scenario — presented as a clear visual choice.

## Tone & Mood

Scientifically precise, urgently serious, and solution-oriented. The tone communicates: the data is clear, the timeline is known, and the choice between scenarios is real. There is scientific integrity in acknowledging uncertainty while communicating the weight of the evidence. Audiences understand what is at stake and what is possible.

## Avoid

- Alarmism without uncertainty acknowledgment
- Oversimplification of complex climate projections
- Cherry-picked data ranges
- Missing uncertainty ranges on projections
- Color scales that distort temperature perception
- Conflation of weather and climate

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in IPCC climate data visualization style. Use a clean white background, a two-panel scientific figure: Panel A showing global mean temperature anomaly as a time series from 1850–2024 in black with ±1σ uncertainty shading in gray, extending to 2100 with three scenario lines (SSP1-2.6, SSP2-4.5, SSP5-8.5) in blue, amber, and red respectively with uncertainty ranges, horizontal reference lines at +1.5°C and +2°C Paris targets labeled; Panel B showing a spatial heatmap of regional temperature projections. Include IPCC-style panel labels (A, B), scenario legend with full names, uncertainty qualifier language, data source citation. The feel should be scientifically authoritative, urgently serious, and solution-aware.
