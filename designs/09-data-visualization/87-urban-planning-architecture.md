# Design 87: Urban Planning & Architecture

**Category:** Data & Visualization  
**Style Tags:** urban-planning, architecture, axonometric, blueprint, site-plan, spatial  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Urban planning presentations, architectural project briefings, real estate development pitches, city policy presentations, and any spatial design content. Ideal for urban planners, architects, and real estate developers.

---

## Visual Direction

Architectural drawing conventions, urban planning site plan aesthetics, axonometric projection views, blueprint technical drawing on cyan-tinted paper, and the visual vocabulary of professional design and planning practice. The design communicates spatial thinking — understanding the world through its physical dimensions and relationships.

## Background & Texture

Blueprint paper blue (#0A1A3A) or drafting vellum (#F5F0E8) — the two primary surfaces of architectural drawing. Blueprint style: white lines on deep blue. Traditional drafting: pencil and ink on vellum. Both communicate technical precision.

## Typography

Clean engineering drawing fonts: Architectural, a monospace or condensed technical sans. Dimension labels in standard architectural notation. "NORTH" with compass arrow as orientation. Scale bar notation ("1:100" or "Scale: 1 inch = 20 feet"). Sheet title block in the lower-right corner following drawing convention.

## Color Palette

**Blueprint Style:**
- **Primary:** Deep blue (#0A1A3A) — blueprint paper
- **Secondary:** White (#FFFFFF) — all linework and annotations

**Drafting Style:**
- **Primary:** Vellum cream (#F5F0E8) — drafting surface
- **Secondary:** Pencil dark (#1A1A14) — linework
- **Accent 1:** Material red (#CC2200) — highlighted elements, proposed new construction
- **Neutral:** Existing gray (#8A8A8A) — existing conditions, context buildings

## Layout & Composition

Plan view (top-down site plan) as the primary composition element. North arrow and scale bar positioned in standard locations. Section/elevation drawings in the lower portion. Detail callout circles indicating enlarged views. Dimension strings along building edges. Room labels and area calculations. Title block in the lower-right corner.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Plan view building outlines with room divisions. Hatching patterns for different materials and construction types. Dimension strings with tick marks at endpoints. Door and window symbols in standard architectural notation. North arrow as compass symbol. Scale bar with multiple scale increments. Contour lines for topographic information. Tree and vegetation plan symbols. Parking lot striping. Road right-of-way lines.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Site plan or axonometric view of the project, project title in title block format, client name, project number, date, drawing set.
- **Content Slide:** Plan view with called-out enlarged detail, dimension annotations, material legend.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Site development phasing diagram — Phase 1, 2, 3 in different hatching patterns on the same site plan, each phase progressively added.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Area analysis table with program areas, percentages, and FAR calculations, alongside the plan view.
- **Closing Slide:** Axonometric or perspective view of the completed project with key statistics: total square footage, unit count, open space percentage.

## Tone & Mood

Technically precise, spatially thoughtful, and design-rigorous. The tone communicates that every square foot has been thought about carefully and every design decision serves a purpose derivable from the site's constraints and the program's needs. Audiences trust the rigor of the spatial thinking.

## Avoid

- Approximate or imprecise drawings
- Inconsistent architectural drawing conventions
- Decorative elements outside the drafting vocabulary
- Missing scale references
- Drawings that cannot be read by professionals in the field
- 3D visualization where plan views serve the analysis better

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in architectural and urban planning drawing style. Use a blueprint-style deep blue background with white linework showing a site plan of a mixed-use urban block, building outlines in white, interior room division lines at lighter weight, dimension strings along the perimeter and key interior dimensions, a north arrow in the upper-right corner, a 1:100 scale bar below the plan, hatching patterns differentiating residential, retail, and open space areas, a material legend in the lower-left, and a title block in the lower-right with project name, date, drawing number. The feel should be technically precise, architecturally rigorous, and spatially thoughtful — professional planning and architectural drawing in presentation format.
