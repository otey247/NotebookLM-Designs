# Design 23: Harvard Business Review

**Category:** Corporate & Professional  
**Style Tags:** HBR, thought-leadership, business-editorial, research-backed, authoritative, management  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Thought leadership presentations, management research briefings, executive education sessions, and organizational change presentations. Ideal for senior leaders, professors, and management thinkers who want to present ideas with research authority.

---

## Visual Direction

Harvard Business Review's authoritative editorial aesthetic for business — bold opinions backed by research, conceptual illustrations that simplify complex ideas, strong pull quotes from interviewees and researchers, and an editorial voice that bridges academic rigor with executive accessibility. The design communicates that serious research is behind every claim.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) for body content. HBR signature red (#E63329) used boldly in header sections and callout boxes — this is a brand color of intellectual authority. Subtle paper-quality texture at very low opacity suggests the physical magazine.

## Typography

Strong headline serif (Merriweather, Playfair Display, or HBR's own editorial fonts) for display. Clean sans-serif (Source Sans, Roboto) for body text. Bold pull quote in large serif italic. Author attribution in small caps. Section headers in HBR red with rule line. Byline format: "By [First Name] [Last Name] · [Title/Affiliation]".

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** HBR red (#E63329) — brand identity, section labels, callout accents
- **Secondary:** Rich charcoal (#2A2A2A) — primary body text
- **Accent 1:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — background and knockout elements
- **Accent 2:** Deep gray (#5A5A5A) — secondary text, captions, supporting content
- **Neutral:** Warm light gray (#EBEBEB) — sidebar backgrounds, infobox fills

## Layout & Composition

Editorial two-column layout: wider left column for main argument, narrower right column for sidebar data, examples, or framework boxes. Dominant headline spanning full width above. Pull quote floating at column midpoint. Research attribution and "source" boxes at key data points. Conceptual framework diagram in a separate full-width treatment.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

HBR-style conceptual diagrams: simple 2×2 matrices labeled with descriptive axis titles, two-circle Venn diagrams for concept intersection, horizontal maturity curves. Pull quotes in large italic HBR red or charcoal. Sidebar boxes with light gray backgrounds and bold section titles. Author photos in small circles. Bold statistic callout boxes ("73% of executives report...") in HBR red with white text.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** HBR red header bar with publication name and issue, bold serif headline below, author bylines with affiliations, article category label.
- **Content Slide:** Two-column layout with argument body, pull quote at midpoint, research sidebar box with statistic and source.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Conceptual diagram spanning full width (maturity curve, 2×2 matrix, change arc), labeled with descriptive HBR-style axis titles.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Survey result visualization with bold percentage callout, research methodology note, finding framed as management insight.
- **Closing Slide:** "Key Takeaways" header, three bold summary points, "About the Authors" section, article reprint information.

## Tone & Mood

Intellectually confident, research-grounded, and practically oriented. The tone communicates: this analysis is based on real evidence, and it has direct implications for how you manage your organization. Accessible to senior executives without sacrificing analytical substance.

## Avoid

- Decorative visual elements without intellectual purpose
- Casual or informal typography
- Data presented without research context
- Bullet point lists (use insight statements and prose fragments)
- Bright colors beyond HBR red and charcoal
- Generic corporate iconography

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in Harvard Business Review editorial style. Use HBR signature red (#E63329) as the primary accent color in a header section and callout boxes, a clean white body background, bold serif headline spanning full width, two-column editorial layout with the main argument on the left and a research sidebar on the right, a large italic pull quote in HBR red with byline attribution, a conceptual 2×2 matrix diagram with descriptive axis titles, and a bold statistic callout box ("73% of executives...") in HBR red with white knockout text. The feel should be intellectually authoritative, research-grounded, and editorially confident.
