# Design 27: Legal & Compliance Briefing

**Category:** Corporate & Professional  
**Style Tags:** legal, compliance, risk, regulatory, formal, meticulous  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Legal compliance training, regulatory update briefings, risk management presentations, HR policy rollouts, and any context where precision, defensibility, and documented accountability matter. Ideal for legal departments, compliance officers, and governance teams.

---

## Visual Direction

Law firm and regulatory compliance presentation aesthetics — formal, meticulous, and structurally airtight. The design communicates: this information is accurate, its legal basis is documented, and the organization is taking its obligations seriously. No element of casual creativity — only the seriousness of legal precision.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) or very light gray (#F8F8F8) — clinical precision. Dark navy header bar with section number and title. No decorative texture. Optional subtle watermark ("CONFIDENTIAL — ATTORNEY-CLIENT PRIVILEGE" or "INTERNAL USE ONLY") at very low opacity in the background.

## Typography

Traditional legal serif (Times New Roman, Garamond) for body text and formal sections, or a clean institutional sans-serif (Arial) for more modern legal compliance contexts. Section numbers in bold: "Section 3.2 | Compliance Requirements". Bold for regulatory citations ("Per 17 CFR § 229.101"). Footnotes in smaller type with full legal citations. Document control information (version, date, author) in footer.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Deep navy (#1A2A4A) — structural headers, section bars
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — body background
- **Accent 1:** Compliance green (#1F6B35) — compliant status, approved items
- **Accent 2:** Non-compliance red (#9B1A1A) — violations, prohibited items, critical risks
- **Neutral:** Amber (#D97706) — caution items, areas requiring monitoring

## Layout & Composition

Formal two-section layout: header zone with section number and title in the navy bar, body zone with structured content. Numbered clauses or requirements listed with proper legal formatting. Risk matrix tables with likelihood and impact columns. Compliance checklist format with checkboxes. Flowchart of legal decision processes. Clear visual separation between normative requirements and guidance.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Compliance status checkboxes (☑ compliant, ☒ non-compliant, ○ N/A). Risk rating matrices (3×3 likelihood × impact grids) with color-coded cells. Legal citation footnotes. Process flowcharts with diamond decision nodes. Section divider bars with regulation reference numbers. Regulatory body logos (SEC, FDA, GDPR Commission) where relevant. Document version control information.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Organization name, policy or regulation title, version number, effective date, document owner, "CONFIDENTIAL" designation.
- **Content Slide:** Section header with regulation citation, numbered requirement clauses, compliance status indicators, responsible party assignment.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Legal decision flowchart — "Is X required?" → Yes/No branches → required action steps → compliance confirmation.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Risk heat map (likelihood × impact), compliance status summary bar chart, pending action items with due dates.
- **Closing Slide:** Compliance acknowledgment checklist, certification statement, signature/attestation block, next review date.

## Tone & Mood

Meticulous, risk-aware, and formally accountable. The tone communicates organizational seriousness about its legal and regulatory obligations. Every statement is precise, every claim is citable, and every risk is acknowledged and managed. Audiences understand exactly what is required of them.

## Avoid

- Casual or informal visual language
- Imprecise or hedged language in any context
- Decoration without informational function
- Inconsistent formatting (legal presentations are audited for consistency)
- Missing citations or unsupported claims
- Visual elements that could be misinterpreted as instructions or regulations

## Example Prompt

> Design a legal and compliance briefing presentation slide. Use a clean white background with a deep navy header bar containing the section number and regulation citation title, numbered requirement clauses in formal serif body text with regulatory citation footnotes, a compliance status table with green (compliant), amber (monitoring required), and red (non-compliant) indicators, a 3×3 risk likelihood × impact heat map with color-coded severity cells, document version and effective date in the footer, and a "CONFIDENTIAL — INTERNAL USE ONLY" watermark at low opacity. The feel should be meticulous, formally accountable, and legally precise.
