# Design 24: Enterprise SaaS Pitch Deck

**Category:** Corporate & Professional  
**Style Tags:** SaaS, B2B, enterprise, pitch-deck, product, trust-building  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** B2B sales presentations, enterprise software demos, investor pitch decks, and vendor evaluation materials. Ideal for SaaS companies, technology vendors, and startup founders presenting to corporate buyers or investors.

---

## Visual Direction

Salesforce, Workday, and ServiceNow sales presentation aesthetics — product-forward, benefit-clear, risk-reducing, and trust-signaling. The design communicates: this is an established, reliable technology platform with proven ROI and enterprise-grade security. Every visual element builds toward reducing buyer anxiety and increasing confidence.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) for content slides. Solid corporate blue (#0066CC or brand equivalent) for section dividers and title slides. Subtle light gray (#F7F8FA) for card backgrounds and infoboxes. No texture — enterprise SaaS signals digital precision through clean surfaces.

## Typography

Clean, accessible sans-serif system font or brand font (Salesforce Sans, Lato, or similar). Bold for headlines, regular for body, light for supporting text. Consistent typographic scale. Button-style CTA labels in all-caps. Feature category labels in bold small caps. Numbered steps in bold.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Corporate blue (#0066CC) — primary brand color, CTAs, headers
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — background, card surfaces
- **Accent 1:** Success green (#28A745) — outcomes, ROI highlights, positive metrics
- **Accent 2:** Amber (#F59E0B) — attention items, feature highlights, callouts
- **Neutral:** Light gray (#F3F4F6) — card backgrounds, table rows, section dividers

## Layout & Composition

Card-based grid layout — 2×2 or 3-column grids of feature cards. Clean four-quadrant frameworks for market positioning. Timeline lanes for implementation phases. Split-screen hero layouts (product screenshot left, benefit text right). Table-based comparison views. Consistent spacing and alignment throughout.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Product UI screenshots (or mockup placeholders) as primary visual elements. Customer logo walls (three or four recognizable logos). Security and compliance badges (SOC 2, ISO 27001, GDPR icons). Integration logos showing ecosystem connectivity. Simple circular process diagrams. ROI calculation showcases with dollar figure callouts. Quote cards with customer headshots and testimonials.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Company logo and tagline, hero product screenshot, main value proposition headline, customer logo strip at the bottom.
- **Content Slide:** Feature grid (2×2 or 3-column cards), each card with icon, bold feature name, and 2-sentence benefit description.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Implementation timeline with phase labels (Discovery → Configure → Deploy → Adopt), estimated timelines, and milestone markers.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** ROI calculator visualization with customer input assumptions and output metrics, before/after comparison bars.
- **Closing Slide:** Pricing tier comparison table, next steps CTA box, security/compliance badge row, contact information.

## Tone & Mood

Confident, professional, and reassuring. The tone communicates: we have done this before, it works, and we will make it work for you. Risk reduction is as important as feature showcasing. The audience should feel safer about buying this product by the end of the presentation.

## Avoid

- Hand-drawn or rough visual elements
- Aggressive or confrontational language
- Amateur-looking charts or diagrams
- Overly technical jargon without plain-language translation
- Dark mode (enterprise buyers prefer light mode for sales presentations)
- Pricing before value is established

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in enterprise SaaS pitch deck style inspired by Salesforce and Workday. Use a clean white background with corporate blue (#0066CC) as the primary brand color, a 3-column feature card grid with icons, bold feature names, and short benefit descriptions, a product UI screenshot placeholder in a card frame with light gray background, security compliance badges (SOC 2, ISO 27001) in a row at the bottom, customer logo strip above the badges, and a bold ROI metric callout box in success green. The feel should be professional, trust-building, and enterprise-credible — the visual language of an established B2B technology platform.
