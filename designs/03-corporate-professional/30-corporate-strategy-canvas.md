# Design 30: Corporate Strategy Canvas

**Category:** Corporate & Professional  
**Style Tags:** strategy, workshop, canvas, business-model, collaborative, visual-thinking  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Strategy workshops, business model innovation sessions, competitive landscape reviews, value proposition design sessions, and organizational design workshops. Ideal for strategy teams, business development professionals, and facilitators.

---

## Visual Direction

Business model canvas, strategy maps, and value chain analysis aesthetics applied to presentation design. Large visual frameworks with labeled zones, sticky note–style input areas, and customer journey overlays. The design facilitates strategic thinking rather than just presenting conclusions — it shows the framework and invites engagement with it.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) as the primary background. Light gray (#F3F4F6) as canvas zone backgrounds. Strategic frameworks rendered as large bordered rectangle grids taking up most of the slide. Optional subtle grid dot pattern at very low opacity for a workshop whiteboard feeling.

## Typography

Clean, friendly sans-serif (Lato, Inter, or Nunito) — approachable and collaborative rather than formal. Bold headers for zone labels in frameworks. Smaller regular weight for content within zones. Handwritten-style annotations (Caveat, Patrick Hand, or similar) for sticky note text and informal callouts.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Corporate blue (#2563EB) — structural framework borders and headers
- **Secondary:** White (#FFFFFF) — background and canvas surface
- **Accent 1:** Opportunity orange (#F97316) — opportunity zones, bold insights
- **Accent 2:** Risk red (#EF4444) — threat zones, risk callouts
- **Neutral:** Strategy gray (#9CA3AF) — framework grid lines, supporting elements

## Layout & Composition

Large framework grid taking 70–80% of the slide surface. Framework zones (Business Model Canvas: 9 zones; Value Chain: 5 primary + 4 support; 2×2 competitive matrix: 4 quadrants) as the primary structure. Sticky note elements (small colored rectangles with handwritten-style text) populate the zones. An insight callout or synthesis box appears outside the framework summarizing the key strategic finding.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Colored sticky note rectangles with handwritten-style text as framework inputs. Bold framework zone labels (e.g., "VALUE PROPOSITIONS", "CUSTOMER SEGMENTS"). Connecting arrows between related zones. Strategy synthesis callout box outside the main framework. Customer journey swim lane if applicable. Importance ranking dots (●●●○○ scales) for prioritization.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Framework title ("Business Model Canvas: [Company Name]"), date, workshop participants, strategic question being addressed.
- **Content Slide:** Full Business Model Canvas or similar framework grid with populated sticky notes in key zones and connecting arrows showing relationships.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Customer journey map lane diagram — from awareness to advocacy — with touchpoints, emotions, and pain points in parallel horizontal lanes.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Competitive position 2×2 matrix with company positions plotted and strategic movement arrows.
- **Closing Slide:** Strategic synthesis — top 3 insights from the canvas exercise, priority actions, and next steps for strategy refinement.

## Tone & Mood

Collaborative, visual-thinking, and practically oriented. The tone communicates: strategy is a team sport and the thinking should be visible, not just the conclusions. The design invites participation and iteration. Audiences feel engaged as co-creators rather than passive recipients.

## Avoid

- Dense text without visual framework structure
- Formal corporate visual language that discourages participation
- Static data charts without strategic interpretation
- Multiple competing frameworks on one slide
- Small font sizes that prevent visual scanning
- Closed, conclusion-only presentations without the working framework visible

## Example Prompt

> Design a strategy canvas presentation slide featuring a Business Model Canvas framework. Use a clean white background with a large 9-zone bordered grid taking 75% of the slide surface, each zone labeled with bold sans-serif headers (Value Propositions, Customer Segments, Channels, etc.), colored sticky note rectangles with handwritten-style text populating the key zones, connecting arrows between related zones, an insight synthesis callout box to the right summarizing the top strategic finding, and a consistent blue and orange accent color scheme distinguishing opportunity zones from risk zones. The feel should be visual-thinking, workshop-collaborative, and strategically engaging.
