# Design 22: Financial Services Report

**Category:** Corporate & Professional  
**Style Tags:** financial, investment-banking, data-heavy, institutional, charts, research-report  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Investor presentations, quarterly business reviews, financial analysis briefings, and market research reports. Ideal for finance teams, investment professionals, and anyone presenting quantitative business intelligence to financial audiences.

---

## Visual Direction

Goldman Sachs, JP Morgan, and Bloomberg research report aesthetics — dense information architecture, financial data visualization standards, institutional credibility signals. The design prioritizes data fidelity and analytical clarity above all else. Visual polish serves information access, not aesthetic impression.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) for report body sections. Deep navy (#0C2340) header and footer bars for institutional framing. No texture or decorative background. Strict page margins and consistent spacing throughout.

## Typography

Clean institutional sans-serif — Arial or Calibri for body, bold for headlines. Financial notation: proper decimal alignment in tables, consistent significant figures, superscript footnote numbers. Small-cap labels for chart axes, column headers, and data sources. Report title in deep navy, section headers in navy with rule line beneath.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Deep institutional navy (#0C2340) — headers, structural elements
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — body background
- **Accent 1:** Positive green (#1A7A4A) — positive returns, gains, upward trends
- **Accent 2:** Alert red (#CC2200) — losses, risks, downward trends, warnings
- **Neutral:** Mid gray (#8A8A8A) — grid lines, secondary data series, neutral indicators

## Layout & Composition

Header bar with firm logo, report title, date, and analyst names. Footer bar with page number, legal disclaimer reference, and firm information. Body in two columns for research reports or single column for executive summaries. Charts and tables embedded inline. Clear section numbering. Data sources consistently footnoted.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Financial-grade charts: line charts with multiple series, bar charts with data labels, scatter plots with trend lines, waterfall charts for variance analysis. Data tables with alternating row shading, bold header rows, and decimal-aligned numbers. P&L statement format grids. Traffic light performance indicators. Ticker symbol formatting. Date range selectors. Index labels.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Navy header with firm name and division, centered report title in white, client or ticker symbol, date and analyst names below.
- **Content Slide:** Section heading with navy rule, body analysis text, supporting chart with axis labels and data source, footnotes at bottom.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Deal process or investment thesis flowchart, milestone dates, gate decision points.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Primary financial chart at 60%, key metrics summary table beside it, bold findings box with color-coded indicators.
- **Closing Slide:** Summary thesis or rating box, price target table, risk factors list, legal disclaimer.

## Tone & Mood

Precise, trustworthy, and institutionally credible. The tone communicates: this analysis is rigorous, the numbers are accurate, and the conclusions are defensible to regulators and clients. There is no room for visual flourish — every pixel serves the data.

## Avoid

- Decorative visual elements without data function
- Bright or warm accent colors
- Non-standard chart types that obscure rather than reveal data
- Inconsistent number formatting or decimal alignment
- Typography that signals creativity rather than precision
- Gradients on data visualization elements

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in financial services report style inspired by Goldman Sachs and JP Morgan research presentations. Use a deep navy header bar with firm name and report title in white, a clean white body background, dense financial data in a two-column layout with a multi-series line chart showing performance over time, an alternating-row data table with decimal-aligned numbers beside the chart, positive performance highlighted in institutional green and negative in alert red, footnote references at the bottom, and a footer bar with page number and legal disclaimer reference. The feel should be institutional, data-precise, and analytically credible.
