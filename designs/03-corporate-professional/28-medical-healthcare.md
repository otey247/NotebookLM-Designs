# Design 28: Medical & Healthcare Presentation

**Category:** Corporate & Professional  
**Style Tags:** medical, clinical, healthcare, evidence-based, patient-centered, scientific  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Clinical trial results, hospital system presentations, public health briefings, medical education, and pharmaceutical or medtech product presentations. Ideal for physicians, clinical researchers, healthcare executives, and medical educators.

---

## Visual Direction

Clinical trial publications, hospital system strategic presentations, and public health briefing aesthetics. The design communicates evidence-based credibility, patient safety primacy, and scientific rigor. Visual elements directly serve comprehension of clinical data — anatomy diagrams, survival curves, forest plots, and patient journey flows.

## Background & Texture

Clean clinical white (#FFFFFF) — the standard of medical publishing. Light blue-gray (#EEF2F7) for infobox backgrounds. No decorative texture. Medical professional contexts require maximum legibility of data.

## Typography

Clean, accessible sans-serif (Arial, Calibri, or Helvetica) for primary text. Bold for findings and headlines. Smaller precise type for data table contents and footnotes. Medical notation conventions: ± for standard deviation, p-values in italic, N= for sample size, CI= for confidence intervals. Institution name and study title in header.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Medical blue (#1A6EBD) — structural elements, positive outcomes, informational
- **Secondary:** Clinical white (#FFFFFF) — primary background
- **Accent 1:** Positive green (#1A7A4A) — favorable outcomes, effective treatments
- **Accent 2:** Caution amber (#D97706) — monitoring required, side effect flag
- **Neutral:** Adverse red (#CC2200) — adverse events, contraindications, critical findings

## Layout & Composition

Clinical study presentation structure: background/rationale, methods, results, conclusions. One primary data visualization per slide (Kaplan-Meier survival curve, forest plot, bar chart of endpoints). Key findings highlighted as bold text callout near the primary visual. Patient population summary table. Institutional logos and IRB approval references.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Kaplan-Meier survival curves with event tables beneath. Forest plot meta-analysis visualizations. Patient flow diagrams (CONSORT-style enrollment flowcharts). Anatomical diagram placeholders with labeled callouts. Side effect profile tables. Dosing schedule timelines. Patient satisfaction rating scales. IRB/ethics approval reference boxes. ClinicalTrials.gov registration numbers.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Study title, principal investigator(s), institutional affiliations, conference/meeting name, date, and clinical trial registration number.
- **Content Slide:** Background and rationale section — disease burden statistics, unmet need, study hypothesis, primary endpoint definition.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** CONSORT enrollment flowchart or study design schematic with treatment arms, randomization, and follow-up periods.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Primary endpoint Kaplan-Meier curve with median survival times, p-value, and hazard ratio callout; event table beneath.
- **Closing Slide:** Summary of key findings, clinical implications, study limitations, ongoing research, and acknowledgments.

## Tone & Mood

Evidence-first, patient-centered, and scientifically accountable. The tone communicates: this data was gathered rigorously, interpreted carefully, and the implications for patient care are specific and important. Safety is the primary value, efficacy is the second. Statistical honesty is non-negotiable.

## Avoid

- Stock photography of healthy smiling patients (in clinical contexts)
- Over-interpretation of data or p-value misrepresentation
- Decorative visual elements that could distract from clinical data
- Inconsistent statistical notation
- Promotional language without supporting evidence
- Visual elements that oversimplify complex clinical findings

## Example Prompt

> Design a medical and healthcare presentation slide for a clinical trial results presentation. Use a clinical white background with medical blue as the primary accent color, a Kaplan-Meier survival curve as the primary visual element with the event table beneath it, a bold finding callout box highlighting the hazard ratio and p-value, a patient population summary table with baseline characteristics, an institutional affiliation header, and a clinical trial registration number reference. Include proper statistical notation conventions (p-value, CI, N=). The feel should be evidence-first, scientifically rigorous, and clinically precise — appropriate for a medical conference presentation.
