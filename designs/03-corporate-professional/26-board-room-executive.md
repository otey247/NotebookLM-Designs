# Design 26: Board Room Executive Briefing

**Category:** Corporate & Professional  
**Style Tags:** board, executive, C-suite, dense, KPI, strategic-review  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** Board of directors presentations, C-suite strategic reviews, investor update briefings, and quarterly business reviews where maximum information density and decision-facilitating clarity are required.

---

## Visual Direction

Executive board presentation aesthetics — maximum information density balanced with clear signal hierarchy. Every slide answers a decision question: What is the status? What are the key risks? What decision is required? The design prioritizes executive time efficiency — a board member should understand the most important information on any slide within 10 seconds.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) with a thin dark gray or navy header bar at the top containing the slide title as a short, direct statement. Tight but consistent margins. No decorative texture.

## Typography

Small but precise sans-serif system font (Arial, Calibri, or Trebuchet). Bold for key findings and section headers. Smaller than standard — this is a dense format requiring tight typography. Numbers and KPIs in bold at larger scale as visual anchors. Traffic light symbols (●●●) as quick-read status indicators. "CONFIDENTIAL" watermark in light gray diagonally.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Dark charcoal (#2A2A2A) — all text on white backgrounds
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — body background
- **Accent 1:** Status green (#22AA44) — on-track, positive performance
- **Accent 2:** Status red (#CC2200) — at-risk, critical attention required
- **Neutral:** Status amber (#F59E0B) — watch items, moderate risk

## Layout & Composition

Maximally dense but structured. Three or four primary information zones per slide. Traffic light KPI row near the top for instant status summary. Data tables with sorted rows and highlighted outliers. Strategic options matrix in lower zone. Executive summary ("Key Message") box at the top, with all supporting data below serving that message.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Traffic light circles (green/amber/red) as the primary decision-support visual language. Horizontal KPI bar comparing actual vs. target. Simple trend arrows (↑↓→) beside key metrics. "DECISION REQUIRED" callout boxes in amber. Risk heat maps (likelihood × impact 3×3 grids). Gantt-style timeline bars for project status. "APPENDIX REFERENCE: P.12" footnotes.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Confidential stamp, meeting title, date, attendee list (as board presentation cover page).
- **Content Slide:** Bold insight headline at top (the "so what"), three or four KPI tiles with traffic lights, supporting table with variance analysis, key risk noted.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Strategic initiative status swimlane — phases, milestones, owners, red/amber/green RAG ratings.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Dual-axis performance chart (revenue and margin), data table with actuals/budget/variance, bold variance callouts in red.
- **Closing Slide:** Decision agenda — three decisions required from the board, each with options, recommendation, and financial implication.

## Tone & Mood

Precise, direct, and decision-oriented. The tone assumes the audience is intelligent, time-constrained, and skeptical. Every claim should be supported by visible evidence on the same slide. The design facilitates governance — it creates accountability through clarity.

## Avoid

- Decorative visual elements that use space without adding information
- Vague language or hedging in headlines
- Charts without data sources
- Inconsistent status color coding
- Slides that require verbal explanation to understand (board presentations are often reviewed before meetings)
- Overly designed aesthetics that signal more attention to format than content

## Example Prompt

> Design a board room executive briefing slide. Use a clean white background with a dark charcoal header bar containing a direct insight statement as the slide title, a traffic light KPI summary row (green/amber/red circles) for six key metrics with brief labels, a detailed performance table with actual/target/variance columns and highlighted negative variances in red, a dual-axis trend chart showing two performance series, an amber "DECISION REQUIRED" callout box with the specific question, and a "CONFIDENTIAL" watermark at low opacity. The feel should be maximally informative, decision-facilitating, and executive-grade — designed for the board room.
