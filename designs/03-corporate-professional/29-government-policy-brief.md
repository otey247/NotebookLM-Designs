# Design 29: Government Policy Brief

**Category:** Corporate & Professional  
**Style Tags:** government, policy, public-sector, institutional, federal, civic  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Congressional briefings, government agency reports, public policy presentations, advocacy materials for legislators, and civic technology program presentations. Ideal for policy professionals, government affairs teams, and public-sector organizations.

---

## Visual Direction

Federal government agency report aesthetics — institutional typography, seal/emblem placement, flag color references, numbered policy recommendations, cost-benefit analysis tables, and the visual gravitas of documents that carry governmental authority. The design communicates: this is an official analysis with public accountability.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) — government documents prioritize clarity and reproduction quality. Deep navy header bar with agency seal or logo. American flag color palette (red, white, and blue) as the structural color reference. Optional "FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY" or "DRAFT" watermark.

## Typography

Formal sans-serif (Helvetica, Arial, or Times New Roman) matching government agency standards. Section numbered in legal outline format: I., II., A., B., 1., 2. Bold for policy recommendations. Italic for definition boxes. Government-style citations (Public Law 112-23, 42 U.S.C. § 1234, Executive Order 12333). Footer with agency name, document number, and date.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Federal blue (#003087) — header bars, structural elements
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — body background
- **Accent 1:** American red (#BF0A30) — urgency callouts, critical policy items
- **Accent 2:** Government gold (#FFD700) — select emphasis, official seal accent
- **Neutral:** Official gray (#5C5C5C) — secondary text, table borders, supporting content

## Layout & Composition

Agency seal or logo top-left corner. Document title and classification banner. Numbered sections with formal outline hierarchy. Policy recommendation boxes prominently highlighted. Cost-benefit analysis table. Regional or demographic impact maps. Appendix reference footnotes.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Government agency seal or emblem placeholder. American flag color accent bars. Formal outline numbering system. Policy recommendation highlighted boxes ("RECOMMENDATION 1:"). Cost-benefit summary tables. Geographic impact maps showing affected regions or demographics. Approval signature blocks. Classification or distribution markings.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Agency seal, full policy title, document number, date, author/agency name, and distribution classification.
- **Content Slide:** Section header with formal numbering, structured policy text with numbered subsections, supporting data table, legislative citation footnotes.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Policy implementation timeline — legislation → rulemaking → implementation → evaluation phases with milestone dates.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Cost-benefit analysis table with NPV calculation, bar chart of affected populations, geographic distribution map.
- **Closing Slide:** Numbered policy recommendations summary (1–5), immediate next steps, required legislation or executive action, contact information.

## Tone & Mood

Formally authoritative, publicly accountable, and bipartisan-credible. The tone communicates that this analysis serves the public interest, is grounded in statute and evidence, and is subject to the full accountability of public-sector governance. Non-partisan in tone; rigorous in method.

## Avoid

- Partisan political signals or messaging
- Informal or casual typography
- Bright commercial-feeling colors
- Decorative elements without informational function
- Undocumented claims or statistics without citations
- Corporate-style visual design language

## Example Prompt

> Design a government policy brief presentation slide. Use a clean white background with a deep federal blue (#003087) header bar containing the agency name and document number, formal section numbering using legal outline format, numbered policy recommendation boxes highlighted in the body, a cost-benefit analysis summary table with NPV figures and affected population counts, American flag color accent elements (blue and red), an agency seal placeholder in the header, and footer with document number, date, and distribution classification. The feel should be formally authoritative, publicly accountable, and institutionally credible — the visual language of federal government policy analysis.
