# Design 62: Islamic Geometric Pattern

**Category:** Cultural & Aesthetic  
**Style Tags:** Islamic, geometric, mosaic, tilework, arabesque, mathematical  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Middle Eastern and North African cultural presentations, Islamic architecture topics, geometric mathematics education, cross-cultural design presentations, and heritage organization materials. Ideal for cultural institutions and organizations with Middle Eastern cultural contexts.

---

## Visual Direction

Islamic geometric art from the golden age of Islamic architecture — the interlocking star and polygon patterns of Moroccan zellige tilework, Persian Safavid mosque patterns, Moorish Alhambra arabesque, and Mughal architectural ornament. The design communicates geometric beauty, mathematical precision, and spiritual intention simultaneously.

## Background & Texture

Deep lapis lazuli blue (#1A2A6C), deep turquoise (#006B6B), or terracotta (#C4602A) as the primary surface — the palette of Moroccan and Andalusian tilework. The geometric pattern is the background texture, not just a decorative border. Pattern should cover the entire background surface at medium opacity, with foreground elements appearing in front of the pattern.

## Typography

Clean, refined sans-serif for presentation text — the Arabic geometric aesthetic pairs well with clean Western type. Arabic calligraphy as a decorative heading element when contextually appropriate. Body text in a clean legible font at high contrast against the patterned background. White or warm cream on dark pattern backgrounds.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Lapis lazuli blue (#1A2A6C) — primary field color in the tilework
- **Secondary:** Warm cream (#F5EDDA) — text color and light field in the pattern
- **Accent 1:** Terracotta orange (#C4602A) — warm accent tile in pattern
- **Accent 2:** Turquoise (#006B6B) — cool complement in pattern
- **Neutral:** Gold (#C4922A) — connecting tile and accent outline

## Layout & Composition

The geometric pattern occupies the full background. Content appears in white or cream panels that float over the pattern — their clean geometry creating dialogue with the decorative geometry beneath. Central medallion format for title slides (the central rosette of Islamic geometric design as the composition anchor). Content positioned in the four quadrants around the central medallion.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

8-pointed star (khatam) geometric construction. 12-fold rosette pattern. Interlocking polygon pattern (hexagonal, octagonal, or 5-fold). Arabesque vine scroll as border element. Muqarnas honeycomb vault pattern. Central medallion as composition anchor. Geometric border frames in tilework style. Hand-drawn compass and straightedge construction lines (optional mathematical reference).

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Full pattern background in deep blue, central medallion composition with title in cream on a translucent panel, organization name in smaller type below, decorative geometric border frame.
- **Content Slide:** Pattern background, clean cream content panel with rounded or geometric cutout shape, body text in legible font, geometric accent border.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Sequential stages as tiles in a pattern sequence — each tile reveals the next step, building toward a complete pattern representing the complete process.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Data visualization framed within geometric shapes — bar chart elements styled as tiles, pie chart as a geometric rosette.
- **Closing Slide:** Full medallion pattern composition, closing statement in the central medallion, pattern completing to full coverage as the final image.

## Tone & Mood

Geometrically precise, culturally respectful, and mathematically beautiful. The tone communicates that order, beauty, and intellectual precision are unified in this aesthetic tradition. Audiences feel the depth of a design tradition that preceded modern mathematics by centuries and arrived at the same truths through different methods.

## Avoid

- Pastiche or superficial use of Islamic geometric patterns
- Mixing Islamic geometric motifs with culturally inappropriate content
- Reducing the pattern to mere decoration without appreciation for its mathematical structure
- Non-geometric or organic visual elements that clash with the geometric system
- Color palettes outside the traditional tilework range
- Using sacred calligraphic text without appropriate cultural context

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide using Islamic geometric pattern design. Create a full-background pattern in deep lapis lazuli blue (#1A2A6C) using an interlocking 8-pointed star and 12-fold rosette geometric construction in cream, terracotta, and gold — the Moroccan zellige tilework palette. Float a clean cream content panel over the pattern with the presentation title in dark blue, surrounded by a geometric border frame consistent with the pattern vocabulary. Center the composition on a large rosette medallion. The feel should be geometrically precise, culturally reverent, and mathematically beautiful — Islamic geometric art tradition in presentation design.
