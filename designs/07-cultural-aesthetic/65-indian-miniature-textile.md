# Design 65: Indian Miniature & Textile

**Category:** Cultural & Aesthetic  
**Style Tags:** Indian, miniature-painting, textile, Mughal, rangoli, vibrant  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** South Asian cultural presentations, Indian brand heritage content, yoga and wellness presentations, textile and fashion industry content for South Asian markets, and any context celebrating Indian visual traditions. Ideal for cultural organizations and Indian-market brands.

---

## Visual Direction

Mughal miniature painting detail, Rajasthani block print textile patterns, rangoli geometric patterns, Kerala mural compositions, and the rich decorative vocabulary of Indian visual arts. Intricate details at human scale, jewel-toned color saturation, symmetrical mandala-based compositions, and the philosophical depth of a tradition that uses visual beauty as a form of spiritual practice.

## Background & Texture

Deep jewel tones as primary surface: sapphire (#1A1A6C), deep jade (#006B40), rich burgundy (#6A0A2A), or saffron (#C4602A). Block-print textile pattern as background texture at medium opacity — the repeating buteh (paisley) pattern, floral spray print, or geometric block print. The background should feel like a luxurious textile surface.

## Typography

Elegant, refined serif with some calligraphic quality for headlines — Devanagari-inspired letterforms even in Roman scripts, or classical serif with fluid connections. Body text in a clean, readable medium weight. Gold or white type on jewel-tone backgrounds. Optional: Devanagari or another Indian script as decorative title element.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Saffron (#C4602A) — the most sacred Indian color, celebration and sacrifice
- **Secondary:** Peacock blue (#1A6B8C) — peacock, Vishnu, Krishna references
- **Accent 1:** Jewel red (#9B1A1A) — auspicious, bridal, sacred
- **Accent 2:** Gold (#C4922A) — divine light, prosperity, Lakshmi's presence
- **Neutral:** Ivory (#F5F0E8) — text panels, light zones within the pattern

## Layout & Composition

Mandala-based symmetrical composition for title slides — radiating geometry from a central point. Border compositions with intricate floral or geometric running border patterns for content slides. Miniature painting "framing device" compositions with decorative border frame and central scene. Vertical composition for portrait-format content within a horizontal slide.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Rangoli geometric patterns as corner elements. Paisley (buteh) motifs in block-print style. Lotus flower as structural element. Peacock feather fan as decorative element. Running floral border patterns. Symmetrical mandala as central composition anchor. Decorative arch or chatri (umbrella spire) as framing device. Gold outline on all decorative elements.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Jewel-tone background with block-print pattern texture, central mandala composition, title in gold refined serif within the mandala, organization name in white below, gold border with floral running pattern.
- **Content Slide:** Textile pattern border frame, content in a clean ivory center panel, gold decorative corners, lotus accent elements.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Sequential stages as rangoli pattern elements — each stage a distinct geometric element in the rangoli progression building toward a complete pattern.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Data in an ivory panel with jewel-tone decorative frame, gold lotus or peacock accent.
- **Closing Slide:** Full mandala composition, closing phrase in gold within the mandala center, border with running floral pattern.

## Tone & Mood

Richly beautiful, spiritually resonant, and culturally deep. The tone communicates the profound aesthetic tradition of a civilization that has used visual beauty as a form of devotion and philosophical expression for millennia. There is both ornate richness and underlying mathematical order. Audiences experience genuine aesthetic pleasure.

## Avoid

- Stereotyping or caricature of Indian cultural symbols
- Mixing sacred symbols from different religious traditions without contextual care
- Oversimplifying the intricate complexity of Indian visual traditions
- Western-style layout applied without cultural understanding
- Garish or inaccurate color combinations
- Reducing the tradition to superficial exoticism

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in Indian miniature and textile design style. Use a deep sapphire blue background with a subtle block-print floral textile pattern texture at medium opacity, a central mandala composition as the compositional anchor with a title in gold refined serif at center, gold decorative border with a running lotus and geometric pattern frame, peacock blue and saffron as accent colors in the border elements, an ivory content panel in the lower third with body text, rangoli-inspired geometric corner ornaments, and gold outline on all decorative elements. The feel should be richly ornate, culturally deep, and aesthetically devotional — Indian visual tradition in presentation design.
