# Design 61: East Asian Ink Painting

**Category:** Cultural & Aesthetic  
**Style Tags:** Chinese-painting, ink-wash, calligraphy, traditional, sumi-e, cultural  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** Cultural organization presentations, Chinese/Japanese/Korean brand presentations, heritage and tradition topics, and any context where Eastern aesthetic values of harmony, process, and natural imagery should frame the content. Ideal for cultural bridges and organizations with East Asian cultural identity.

---

## Visual Direction

Traditional Chinese ink painting (水墨画 shuǐmò huà) and Japanese sumi-e aesthetics — vertical mountain landscapes, bamboo and plum blossom motifs, calligraphic brush strokes as both text and image, the interplay of wet and dry brush technique, the paper grain showing through wash areas, and poetry inscribed on the painted surface.

## Background & Texture

Xuan paper (宣纸) or rice paper warmth (#F5EFE0) — the specific warm white of traditional East Asian painting paper. Subtle fiber grain in the paper texture. The paper should feel absorbent and organic — the surface that receives the ink. Occasional very light wash marks at the edges suggesting ink bleeding into the paper.

## Typography

Chinese calligraphy or calligraphy-inspired letterforms as the primary display element. English text in a refined light-weight sans-serif that doesn't compete with the calligraphic elements. Seal stamp (朱文, zhūwén) text in red as an authentic authentication mark. Poetry or inscription text running vertically on the right edge in the traditional format.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Sumi ink black (#1A1A14) — very warm black, the tone of traditional ink
- **Secondary:** Rice paper white (#F5EFE0) — paper background
- **Accent 1:** Vermillion red (#C8302A) — seal stamps, signature marks, plum blossom
- **Accent 2:** Muted celadon (#8AAA90) — subtle color wash in landscapes
- **Neutral:** Warm ink gray (#8A8070) — diluted ink wash, secondary strokes

## Layout & Composition

Traditional hanging scroll (立轴 lìzhóu) or horizontal handscroll format adapted to landscape slide. Composition follows traditional principles: main subject offset with generous empty space (虚 xū — emptiness as fullness), diagonal mountain compositions leading the eye from lower-right to upper-left, a vertical inscription column on the right edge, and a seal mark at the lower left or right.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Ink wash mountain landscape as primary visual element. Bamboo silhouette in dry-brush technique. Plum blossom branch in fine detailed brushwork. Calligraphic inscription (title or relevant poetry excerpt) running vertically. Red seal stamp impression (2–3 layered seal marks). Brush stroke demonstrating wet-dry ink technique. Misty mountain peaks in diluted ink wash.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Horizontal hanging scroll format — landscape in ink wash occupying 60%, vertical calligraphic title inscription on the right, red seal marks, presentation title in small refined sans below.
- **Content Slide:** Key ink painting motif relevant to the content theme, body text in light-weight sans positioned in the negative space, small red seal accent.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Bamboo segment progression — each bamboo joint as a stage, growing upward from left to right, labeled at each joint.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Statistics presented in minimal form within the ink painting context — numbers as inscribed calligraphic characters alongside a relevant visual.
- **Closing Slide:** Final ink painting composition — mountain or plum blossom — with closing inscription in calligraphic style, seal marks.

## Tone & Mood

Contemplative, culturally rooted, and aesthetically unhurried. The tone communicates deep cultural respect and the values of patience, process, and the beauty of natural form. Audiences experience an aesthetic that has been refined over thousands of years and carries genuine cultural authority.

## Avoid

- Digital-sharp edges on ink elements
- Bright or saturated colors beyond the defined palette
- Western layout conventions
- Typography that competes with calligraphic elements
- Stereotyping or reductive cultural reference
- Multiple competing visual elements (East Asian painting values selective restraint)

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in traditional East Asian ink painting style inspired by Chinese shuǐmò and Japanese sumi-e. Use a rice paper warm white background with subtle paper fiber grain texture, traditional sumi ink wash mountain landscape taking the left 60% of the composition with misty peaks in diluted gray-black wash and a dry-brush foreground, vertical calligraphic title inscription running along the right edge, two or three red seal stamp impressions at lower right, a plum blossom branch in detailed brushwork in one corner, and a small refined light-weight sans-serif caption. The feel should be contemplative, culturally rooted, and aesthetically refined — traditional East Asian ink painting aesthetics in presentation format.
