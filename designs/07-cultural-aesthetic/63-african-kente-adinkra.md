# Design 63: African Kente & Adinkra

**Category:** Cultural & Aesthetic  
**Style Tags:** African, Kente, Adinkra, Ghanaian, symbolic, textile  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** African cultural presentations, Diaspora heritage events, diversity and inclusion program materials, cultural celebration presentations, and any context honoring West African visual traditions. Ideal for cultural organizations, African institutions, and Diaspora communities.

---

## Visual Direction

Ghanaian Kente weaving patterns and Akan Adinkra symbolic vocabulary applied to presentation design — bold geometric horizontal and vertical strips in the Kente tradition, Adinkra symbol system as meaningful graphic elements, the color symbolism of royal Kente (gold, green, red, black), and the cultural authority of a visual tradition that encodes philosophical concepts in visual form.

## Background & Texture

Rich warm cream (#F5EDD8) or deep kente gold (#C4892A) as primary surface. Kente weave pattern as border or background texture element — horizontal and vertical color strip intersections creating the characteristic Kente grid. Subtle textile grain texture suggesting woven fabric.

## Typography

Bold, confident sans-serif for headlines — the directness of Kente's geometric language translates to clean bold type. Body text in a readable medium-weight sans. Kente color accents in typography (gold headlines, red emphasis). Optional: Twi language or descriptive labels for Adinkra symbols.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Kente gold (#C4892A) — royal presence, primary weave color
- **Secondary:** Warm cream (#F5EDD8) — background and light elements
- **Accent 1:** Kente green (#2D6A2A) — earth, growth, renewal
- **Accent 2:** Kente red (#C42A1A) — passion, blood, sacrifice, political strength
- **Neutral:** Kente black (#1A1A1A) — structure in the weave, textual anchor

## Layout & Composition

Kente strip border framing the full slide — horizontal color bands at the top and bottom, vertical bands at the sides, creating a woven frame. Central content zone within this frame. Adinkra symbols positioned as meaningful decorative elements at corners or alongside relevant concepts. Bold headline in the upper central zone. Content in the central field.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Kente strip pattern borders (color alternating bands in geometric weave pattern). Adinkra symbols as meaningful icons — Gye Nyame (God's supremacy), Sankofa (learning from the past), Dwennimmen (strength and humility), Boa Me Na Me Mmoa Wo (cooperation), and others selected for thematic relevance. Geometric diamond and triangle patterns from Kente vocabulary.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Kente strip border frame, gold headline in bold sans-serif centered, a selected Adinkra symbol above the title with its name and meaning, organization name below.
- **Content Slide:** Kente border frame, body content in the central zone, Adinkra symbol in the corner corresponding to the section's theme, label with symbol name and meaning.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Sequential Adinkra symbols along a horizontal flow — each symbol represents a stage concept, labeled with stage name and symbol meaning.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Data in the central zone with Kente pattern border, one Adinkra symbol as a thematic anchor.
- **Closing Slide:** "Sankofa" (learn from the past) or "Onyankopon" (God, supreme spirit) symbol centered, closing statement in gold, Kente frame.

## Tone & Mood

Culturally rooted, philosophically deep, and communally proud. The tone communicates that this visual tradition carries genuine philosophical wisdom — the Adinkra symbols each encode a concept that took generations to articulate. There is dignity and collective identity in every design choice. Audiences from this cultural tradition feel honored; audiences outside it feel genuinely introduced.

## Avoid

- Superficial or purely decorative use without respect for symbolic meanings
- Mixing incompatible African cultural traditions without context
- Color palettes that violate Kente color symbolism
- Typography that clashes with the bold geometric Kente vocabulary
- Using sacred symbols in inappropriate contexts
- Reducing the tradition to mere "pattern decoration"

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide using Ghanaian Kente and Adinkra cultural design. Create a Kente strip pattern border framing the full slide in alternating horizontal bands of gold, green, red, and black — the royal Kente palette. In the central content zone on a warm cream background, place the presentation title in bold gold sans-serif, with a selected Adinkra symbol (Sankofa or Gye Nyame) above the title with its name and philosophical meaning in small caps below it. Position a second Adinkra symbol in one corner. The feel should be culturally rooted, philosophically meaningful, and proudly communal — the Akan visual tradition applied with respect and understanding.
