# Design 07: Documentary Photo Essay

**Category:** Editorial & Print-Inspired  
**Style Tags:** documentary, photojournalism, film, contact-sheet, caption-driven, black-and-white  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Case studies, field reports, impact presentations, organizational histories, and evidence-based storytelling where visual authenticity and on-the-ground credibility are essential. Ideal for NGOs, journalism, and impact-driven organizations.

---

## Visual Direction

Photojournalism layouts that feel like they were assembled in a darkroom and a newsroom simultaneously. Contact sheet grids, film strip borders, caption typography, location stamps, and timestamp markings signal documentary authenticity. The design communicates "this really happened, and we were there." Black and white photography with selective color treatment where a single vivid element breaks the monochrome.

## Background & Texture

Dark charcoal (#1A1A1A) or deep black as the primary slide background — the darkroom aesthetic. Alternatively, warm sepia or aged newspaper beige for sections featuring archival material. Film leader texture patterns (sprocket holes, frame count numbers) appear at the edges as decorative-functional design elements. Light grain texture overlaid at low opacity throughout.

## Typography

Documentary caption typography: small, tight, and informative. Primary captions in a condensed sans-serif (Trade Gothic, Franklin Gothic Condensed). Location stamps and datelines in bold all-caps monospace. Main headlines in a strong newsprint-weight serif or typewriter-style font. Photo credit bylines in tiny italic. Handwritten-style annotations for informal field notes.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Charcoal black (#1A1A1A) — background and dark sections
- **Secondary:** Film silver (#C8C8C8) — primary text on dark backgrounds
- **Accent 1:** Warm sepia (#8B6347) — archival photo treatment, vintage tones
- **Accent 2:** Vivid red (#CC0000) — single selective color element used for maximum impact
- **Neutral:** Mid-gray (#888888) — captions, secondary text, film border elements

## Layout & Composition

Contact sheet grid layout for multi-image slides — 2×3 or 3×4 grids of photo frames with caption strips below each frame. Film strip horizontal layouts with sequential frames showing progression. Single-image "hero shot" slides with full-bleed photography and caption overlaid at the bottom in a semi-transparent dark bar. Location + date stamps positioned like actual film metadata at the margins.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Film strip sprocket hole borders along slide edges. Frame number and roll identifier typography (ROLL 1 · FRAME 12). Location and date stamps ("Lagos, Nigeria · March 2024"). Rubber stamp effect for location identification. Occasional red selective color treatment on a single object in an otherwise black-and-white photo. Editor's crop marks around key images. Magnifying glass icon for close-up detail sections.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Full-bleed black and white hero photograph, white documentary-style title at bottom in bold condensed sans-serif, location stamp and date beneath, film strip border at edges.
- **Content Slide:** 2×3 contact sheet grid with six photo frames, each with a two-line caption below, location stamp at top, filmmaker/photographer credit at bottom.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Horizontal film strip showing sequential stages of a process, each frame captioned, dateline progression visible in the timestamps.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Statistics presented alongside field photography — numbers contextualized by human faces, places, and situations.
- **Closing Slide:** Single portrait-oriented photograph centered, large white documentary title, location and date, photographer credit.

## Tone & Mood

Authentic, grounded, and evidence-rich. The tone communicates: we were there, we witnessed this, and what we found matters. There is dignity in the subject matter and seriousness in the documentation. Audiences feel they are seeing real evidence of real situations.

## Avoid

- Bright, saturated color palettes
- Clean corporate typography
- Decorative illustration
- Stock photography aesthetics (over-lit, staged, uniform)
- Rounded corners or card-style layouts
- Cheerful or celebratory visual energy (unless the subject warrants it)

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in documentary photo essay style inspired by photojournalism and film contact sheets. Use a deep charcoal background with film strip sprocket borders at the edges, a 2×3 contact sheet grid of black-and-white photo placeholders each with a location stamp and caption in condensed sans-serif, a bold newsprint headline at the top, and selective vivid red used once as an accent. Include film roll identification numbers at the margin. The feel should be authentic, grounded, and visually serious — like documentary evidence assembled for a field report.
