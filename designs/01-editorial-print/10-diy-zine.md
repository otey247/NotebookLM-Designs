# Design 10: DIY Zine & Punk Fanzine

**Category:** Editorial & Print-Inspired  
**Style Tags:** zine, punk, DIY, photocopier, collage, counter-culture  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Disruptive idea presentations, community organizing decks, subversive product launches, creative industry pitches, and any presentation where rejecting corporate polish is itself a message. Ideal for creative communities, activist organizations, and counter-cultural brands.

---

## Visual Direction

Photocopied punk zines, cut-and-paste typography, ransom note lettering, hand-stamped graphics, collage layering, Xerox distortion effects — the raw creative energy of self-published counter-culture publishing. Nothing is polished. Everything is intentional. The roughness IS the design. This aesthetic communicates that the ideas are too urgent and too real for corporate graphic design.

## Background & Texture

Photocopier white-gray (#EBEBEB) with visible Xerox noise, toner dropout patches, and copy artifacts. Heavy paper grain texture. Visible fold marks where the "paper" was folded and re-scanned. Optional: a slightly skewed or off-center base that suggests an imperfect photocopy. The texture should make the slide feel genuinely self-produced.

## Typography

Deliberate typographic chaos as aesthetic system: mixed typefaces in a single headline (simulating cut-and-paste ransom note letters), hand-lettered headings, typewriter text for body, rubber stamp text for emphasis, and the occasional word blown up to fill the entire width. Stencil type, distressed fonts, and misaligned baselines are all intentional. No single typeface family — diversity IS the style.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Photocopier black (#1A1A1A) — dominant ink
- **Secondary:** Photocopier gray-white (#EBEBEB) — background
- **Accent 1:** Neon yellow marker (#FFE500) — hand-marker highlights, attention circles
- **Accent 2:** Hot pink marker (#FF3CAC) — secondary hand emphasis, energy bursts
- **Neutral:** Mid gray (#888888) — half-tone patterns, photocopy mid-tones

## Layout & Composition

Intentionally "broken" layout — elements placed at unexpected angles, column rules that don't quite align, overlapping text and images that wouldn't survive a design school critique. The grid exists but is violated deliberately. Columns of different widths. Images pasted over text. Text running into margins. This violation of conventional layout rules communicates urgency and self-determination.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Hand-drawn circles and arrows pointing to key text. Star-burst shapes with handwritten text inside. Rubber stamp impressions. Photocopied image fragments pasted at angles. Black scribble underlines. Correction fluid whiteout patches with replacement text written over them. Cut-and-paste letter borders. Safety pin, skull, anarchy symbol, and other punk iconography used as decorative marks. "COPY AND DISTRIBUTE FREELY" footer.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Ransom-note headline mixing type sizes and faces, hand-drawn border around the whole slide, rubber stamp date, small drawn star-burst sub-headline.
- **Content Slide:** Mixed-column layout with handwritten margin notes, photocopied image fragment, typewriter body text, neon highlight on key phrase.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Hand-drawn numbered steps with circles and arrows, each step on a torn paper scrap effect, scrawled annotations connecting them.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Statistics hand-lettered at large scale, rough bar chart hand-drawn on graph paper, circle and arrow annotations, "LOOK AT THIS NUMBER" hand-scrawl.
- **Closing Slide:** Single bold statement in mixed cut-letter style, hand-drawn frame, "MAKE COPIES" or "PASS IT ON" rubber stamp at the bottom.

## Tone & Mood

Rebellious, urgent, community-powered, and anti-corporate. The tone communicates: we don't need your permission, we don't need your production budget, and these ideas are too important to wait for professional polish. There is genuine creative joy in the rawness. Audiences feel invited into a community, not presented to.

## Avoid

- Clean, polished digital typography
- Consistent alignment or grid adherence
- Gradients or smooth color transitions
- Any element that looks professionally designed
- Corporate color palettes (navy/white/gray)
- Stock photography

## Example Prompt

> Create a presentation slide in DIY punk zine style inspired by 1970s–1980s photocopied fanzines. Use a photocopier gray-white background with heavy Xerox grain and toner dropout artifacts, ransom-note mixed typography combining cut-and-paste letter styles at varying sizes, neon yellow and hot pink hand-marker highlights, rubber stamp impression labels, hand-drawn circles and arrows pointing to key content, a rough scribble border around the slide edges, and typewriter body text with occasional strikethrough corrections. The feel should be urgently raw, counter-cultural, and deliberately anti-polish — self-published creative energy at maximum.
