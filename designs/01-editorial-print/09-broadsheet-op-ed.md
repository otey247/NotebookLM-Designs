# Design 09: Broadsheet Opinion & Commentary

**Category:** Editorial & Print-Inspired  
**Style Tags:** broadsheet, op-ed, opinion, commentary, New-York-Times, authoritative  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Position papers, thought leadership decks, strategic recommendations, and any presentation where the presenter is making a clear, argued case for a particular view. Ideal for policy advocates, executives presenting recommendations, and thought leaders.

---

## Visual Direction

The sober authority of The New York Times, The Guardian, or The Washington Post op-ed page. Serious opinion journalism aesthetics — structured argument layout, prominent byline, pull quotes in large serif type, the visual equivalent of a well-reasoned opinion piece. The design conveys: this person has thought carefully about this, and you should take it seriously.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) with very subtle newsprint grain at near-invisible opacity. Double rule lines (one thick, one thin) as section dividers — the classic broadsheet editorial page separator. No colored background panels — the authority comes from structural clarity, not color.

## Typography

Primary: a classic broadsheet serif — New York Times-style Century, or equivalent (Georgia, Garamond). Byline in bold sans-serif ("By [Name] · [Date] · [Publication]"). Headlines in bold condensed serif at editorial scale — large but not tabloid-screaming. Pull quotes in large italic (2–3× body size) with double rule lines above and below. Body text at a generous, legible size in two columns. Running section headers in small caps.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Rich black (#111111) — all text
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — background
- **Accent 1:** Classic newspaper blue-gray (#3A5A7A) — section headers, rule accents
- **Accent 2:** Editorial red (#A01A1A) — "Opinion" or section label, occasional emphasis
- **Neutral:** Light gray (#CCCCCC) — rule lines, column separators, secondary frames

## Layout & Composition

Classic two-column op-ed layout with a full-width headline above the columns and a byline between headline and body. Pull quote spanning the column gutter at approximately the midpoint. Rule lines above the headline and at the column gutter. Optional small author headshot in a circle or square at the byline. Section label ("OPINION", "COMMENTARY", "ANALYSIS") in small caps above the headline.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Thin and thick rule line combinations as dividers. Small section label banners in editorial red above headlines. Author headshot circle. Pull quote with double rule above and below. Occasional small editorial illustration (simple black-and-white line art). Volume/issue/date information at the bottom in small all-caps. Editor's note box if needed for context.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Section label ("OPINION"), bold condensed headline, author byline with small headshot, date, and newspaper-style rule lines.
- **Content Slide:** Two-column body layout with headline, pull quote at midpoint, byline, and section label at top.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered argument points presented as editorial "subheadings" with brief bold labels and supporting paragraph sentences.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Data embedded in an editorial "infographic" box with a caption, woven into prose context rather than standalone.
- **Closing Slide:** Closing argument in large italic pull quote style, author attribution, brief author bio paragraph.

## Tone & Mood

Authoritative, opinion-forward, and intellectually engaged. The tone is confident without being aggressive — the measured confidence of a skilled columnist who has made arguments like this before and expects to be heard. Audiences feel they are reading a well-reasoned case.

## Avoid

- Colorful decorative backgrounds
- Display headlines at tabloid scale
- Bullet point lists (use prose structure instead)
- Infographic-heavy data presentations (embed data in editorial context)
- Bright or cheerful visual energy
- Any element that looks like a PowerPoint template

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide styled as a serious broadsheet newspaper opinion page from The New York Times or The Guardian. Use a clean white background with double rule lines as dividers, a bold condensed serif headline, a byline in bold sans-serif with publication and date, two-column body text in Georgia or Garamond, a pull quote in large italic spanning the column gutter with rule lines above and below, and a small "OPINION" section label in editorial red above the headline. The feel should be authoritative, measured, and intellectually serious — the visual equivalent of a thoughtfully written op-ed.
