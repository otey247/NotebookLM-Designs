# Design 04: Academic Journal & Research Paper

**Category:** Editorial & Print-Inspired  
**Style Tags:** academic, journal, research, peer-review, scholarly, two-column  
**Difficulty:** Beginner  
**Best For:** Research findings presentations, literature review summaries, evidence-based program evaluations, and academic conference talks. Ideal when credibility and methodological rigor are the primary signals to project.

---

## Visual Direction

Structured, rigorous, and institutional — the visual language of peer-reviewed academic publishing. The design prioritizes information density, citation credibility, and methodological clarity over visual flair. Everything is in service of the argument and the evidence. The aesthetic draws from Nature, PLOS ONE, The Lancet, and similar academic journals.

## Background & Texture

Clean white (#FFFFFF) base with no texture — academic journals do not signal warmth through paper feel, they signal credibility through structural clarity. Light gray (#F4F4F4) background for abstract or infobox sections. No decorative texture.

## Typography

Institutional serif typeface for headlines — Times New Roman, Cambria, or Garamond. Two-column body text in the same serif at 10–11pt equivalent. Section headers in bold small caps. Author and affiliation lines in italic below the title. Footnote and citation text at reduced size. Figure labels (Fig. 1, Table 2) in bold sans-serif for contrast. Abstract box in slightly smaller body type.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Institutional black (#1A1A1A) — all body text and headlines
- **Secondary:** Clean white (#FFFFFF) — background
- **Accent 1:** Academic navy (#1E3A5F) — section header rules, callout boxes
- **Accent 2:** Forest green (#2D6A4F) — positive result indicators, accepted hypothesis markers
- **Neutral:** Light gray (#D0D0D0) — table cell borders, column rules, figure frames

## Layout & Composition

Classic two-column journal article layout adapted to landscape slides. Title and authors span full width at top. Body divides into two equal columns separated by a thin rule line. Figures and tables are embedded inline with captions. An abstract or summary box spans full width below the title in a light gray background. Section headers break the columns with a full-width treatment and rule line below.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Numbered figure labels (Fig. 1a, Fig. 1b) with thin border frames. Data tables with hairline cell borders and alternating row shading. Statistical annotation marks (*, **, *** for significance levels). Citations in superscript. DOI and journal name footer. Institutional affiliation banner at the top. Occasional Venn diagram or conceptual model box within the text.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Full-width title in bold serif, author names and affiliations in italic, abstract paragraph in gray box, journal name and DOI at bottom.
- **Content Slide:** Two-column layout with section header, body paragraphs, inline figure with caption, and footnote references.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Methods flowchart in a centered full-width figure box, labeled with numbered steps and bold figure caption below.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Data figure centered with detailed caption below, statistical significance annotations, a bordered data table alongside.
- **Closing Slide:** "Conclusions" section header, bulleted findings in two columns, "Limitations and Future Work" subsection, full citation list.

## Tone & Mood

Rigorously credible, methodologically serious, and intellectually honest. The tone is that of a scientist or scholar presenting carefully gathered evidence to peers who will scrutinize the methodology. There is no room for aesthetic distraction — the design serves the argument.

## Avoid

- Decorative backgrounds or textures
- Bold color accents without semantic meaning
- Illustrations or clipart
- Large typography or display-scale headlines
- White space as an aesthetic choice (white space is for column structure only)
- Any element that could not appear in a peer-reviewed journal

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide styled as a peer-reviewed academic journal article layout. Use a clean white background, classic two-column body text in Times New Roman or Garamond, a bold serif full-width title at the top with author affiliations in italic, a light gray abstract summary box, section headers in bold small caps with a rule line, and a numbered figure with caption. Include statistical significance markers and superscript citations. The overall feel should be rigorous, institutional, and credibly scholarly — indistinguishable from a published journal article layout.
