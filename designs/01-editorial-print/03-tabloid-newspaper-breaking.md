# Design 03: Tabloid Newspaper Breaking News

**Category:** Editorial & Print-Inspired  
**Style Tags:** tabloid, newspaper, breaking-news, urgent, halftone, high-energy  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Research findings announcements, product launches framed as major events, trend reports with urgent implications. Best when the presenter wants to create immediate attention and high energy around information.

---

## Visual Direction

High-drama newspaper front page energy — screaming bold headlines, urgent red banners, halftone photo effects, column inches, ticker-style information. Every element competes for attention in deliberate tabloid fashion. The design should feel like the most important news of the day has just broken and must be communicated immediately.

## Background & Texture

Slightly off-white newsprint texture — faint gray grain suggesting cheap, fast-printed paper. Visible column grid lines as subtle guides. Optional: slight ink bleed effect at headline edges. Background can include faint ghost text of "lorem ipsum" newspaper columns beneath the main content to reinforce the print newspaper metaphor.

## Typography

Screaming, bold, compressed headlines in an ultra-black condensed typeface (Impact, Franklin Gothic Extra Condensed, or Compacta). Large first-word scale — the first word of a headline can be 3× the size of the rest. Bylines in bold all-caps with "BY [NAME]" format. Body text in a tight, slightly compressed sans-serif at small sizes to pack maximum information. Pull quotes or scandal highlights in bold italic with quotation marks.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Rich black (#0A0A0A) — all headlines and body text
- **Secondary:** Newsprint white-gray (#F0EDE8) — background
- **Accent 1:** Urgent red (#CC0000) — breaking news banners, exclamation callouts
- **Accent 2:** Warning yellow (#FFD700) — highlight boxes and second-tier emphasis
- **Neutral:** Medium gray (#888888) — secondary headlines, captions, column rules

## Layout & Composition

Classic broadsheet front-page grid adapted to landscape slide format. A dominant screaming headline spans the full top. Below: one or two columns of body text alongside a large halftone-treated photo. Red "BREAKING NEWS" or "EXCLUSIVE" banner across the top or diagonally across a photo. Column rule lines separate information zones. Dense and information-packed — the opposite of minimalism.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Halftone dot-pattern photo treatment (CMYK print simulation). Bold red rectangular banners with white knockout text ("BREAKING", "EXCLUSIVE", "DEVELOPING"). Star-burst graphic elements for exclamation emphasis. Ink splatter or smudge effects at the edges. Ticker-tape style information strip along the bottom margin. Optional front-page nameplate/masthead at the very top of the slide.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Giant headline in two sizes, red "BREAKING" banner across photo, masthead at top with date and edition number, ticker strip at bottom.
- **Content Slide:** Three-column mini-article layout, bold headline per column, red accent rules, halftone sidebar image with caption.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered "DEVELOPING STORY" timeline from left to right, each stage in a bordered news brief box with bold mini-headline.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** "THE NUMBERS" callout box with large statistics, newspaper-style infographic with halftone shading, red bars for emphasis.
- **Closing Slide:** "WHAT HAPPENS NEXT" bold banner headline, bulleted "developing story" follow-up list, red editorial final note box.

## Tone & Mood

Urgent, attention-commanding, and deliberately over-the-top in the tradition of tabloid news energy. There is a knowing wink to the aesthetic — it's bold enough to be dramatic without becoming parody. The audience should feel the urgency and importance of the information.

## Avoid

- Soft pastel colors or gentle gradients
- Quiet white space
- Elegant serif body text at readable sizes
- Rounded corners on any element
- Photography without halftone treatment
- Understated typography

## Example Prompt

> Create a presentation slide styled as a dramatic tabloid newspaper front page. Use an off-white newsprint texture background with a visible column grid, an ultra-black compressed headline font at massive scale, a bold red "BREAKING NEWS" banner across a halftone-treated photo placeholder, and dense small body text in column layout. Include a star-burst exclamation element and a ticker-tape information strip along the bottom. The overall feel should be urgent, high-energy, and unapologetically bold — like the most important story of the day has just broken.
