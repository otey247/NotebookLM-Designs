# Design 02: Vintage Magazine Feature

**Category:** Editorial & Print-Inspired  
**Style Tags:** magazine, editorial, photography, multi-column, pull-quote, 1960s  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Narrative-driven presentations, brand storytelling, and cultural or historical topic coverage. Ideal for presenters who want a sophisticated editorial voice that feels curated and authoritative.

---

## Visual Direction

Rich, photographically driven layouts inspired by the golden age of American and European magazines — Life, Time, National Geographic, Rolling Stone, and Vogue. The design balances image-first composition with strong typographic hierarchy. Spread-style layouts with multi-column text evoke the prestige and production quality of long-form print journalism.

## Background & Texture

Clean white or warm cream base with subtle paper grain texture. Thin rule lines may separate columns or frame content zones. Occasional vintage paper tone creeping in at edges suggests a physical printed object. Column gutters are clean and consistent, maintaining legibility across dense content.

## Typography

Strong masthead-style display headline at the top using a bold condensed serif or slab serif. Subheadings in smaller bold serif or italic. Body copy in a refined text serif — Georgia, Palatino, or Garamond — at readable sizes in a multi-column layout. Pull quotes in large italic serif with rule lines above and below, positioned to break the column grid.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Deep navy (#1A2744) — headlines and structural text
- **Secondary:** Warm cream (#F5EFE0) — background and text panels
- **Accent 1:** Burgundy (#8B1A2C) — section labels, captions, and bylines
- **Accent 2:** Warm gold (#C9982A) — pull quote rules, decorative dividers
- **Neutral:** Medium warm gray (#8A7E72) — captions, footnotes, secondary text

## Layout & Composition

Classic magazine spread grid: two or three columns of body text, one large dominant image bleeding to the edge, and one prominent headline spanning at least two columns. Asymmetric but gridded — the layout follows invisible column rules while appearing dynamic through image placement. Pull quotes float at the center or edge of a column break, creating visual rhythm.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Large-format photography placeholders with caption blocks beneath in italic small caps. Horizontal rule lines (hairline to 2pt) used as section dividers. Small editorial stamps or department labels ("COVER STORY", "SPECIAL REPORT") in all-caps with box borders. Page folio numbers and magazine title watermarks at margins. Occasional hand-drawn annotation or editor's mark as a tasteful stylistic flourish.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Full-bleed image taking 60% of the slide, masthead-style title at top, deck number and date in small caps at bottom margin.
- **Content Slide:** Two-column body text layout with pullquote spanning the gutter, header rule, and small department label above headline.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered editorial steps presented as mini-feature sidebar boxes with rule lines and captions, arranged across the slide horizontally.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Chart positioned as a featured "infographic box" with editorial framing caption, data source footnote, and bold summary statistic highlighted.
- **Closing Slide:** Single powerful closing quote in large italic serif, attributed with a byline-style credit, rule lines above and below.

## Tone & Mood

Authoritative, narrative, and sophisticated — the confidence of a seasoned magazine editor who knows exactly what to emphasize and what to leave out. The mood is intellectually engaged, slightly nostalgic, and deeply credible. Audiences feel like they are reading something worth reading.

## Avoid

- Flat corporate color blocks
- Digital UI chrome or rounded cards
- Sans-serif-only typography
- Stock illustration clipart
- Bullet-pointed lists (use body copy paragraphs or numbered editorial sidebars instead)
- Bright neon or primary-color accents

## Example Prompt

> Design a presentation slide in the style of a 1960s American magazine feature spread. Use a warm cream background with subtle paper grain, a bold condensed serif headline spanning the top, a large photography placeholder bleeding to one edge, two-column body text in a refined serif font, and a pull quote with gold rule lines breaking the column grid. Include a small department label in burgundy all-caps and a caption block in italic beneath the image. The feel should be editorially prestigious, narrative-driven, and reminiscent of a Life or Time magazine long-form feature.
