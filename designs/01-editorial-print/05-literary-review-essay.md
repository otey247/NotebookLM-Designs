# Design 05: Literary Review & Essay

**Category:** Editorial & Print-Inspired  
**Style Tags:** literary, essay, New-Yorker, contemplative, serif, long-form  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate  
**Best For:** Thought leadership presentations, cultural analysis, philosophical arguments, and long-form content summaries. Ideal for presenters who write and think in essay form and want their slides to reflect that intellectual register.

---

## Visual Direction

Refined literary aesthetics drawn from The New Yorker, The Paris Review, The London Review of Books, and The Atlantic. Generous margins, unhurried pacing, drop caps, and elegant typographic hierarchy create an atmosphere of intellectual seriousness without academic stuffiness. The design communicates that the ideas here are worth slowing down for.

## Background & Texture

Warm ivory (#FAF6EE) — not pure white, not newsprint gray, but the specific warmth of quality literary magazine paper. Subtle paper grain at very low opacity. Wide margins (15–20% of slide width) on both sides create the breathing room that signals premium long-form publishing.

## Typography

Primary typeface: a refined literary serif — Caslon, Minion Pro, or Sabon. Drop caps on opening paragraphs scaled to 3–4 lines tall. Section titles in italic small caps. Running header in small all-caps with a thin rule beneath. Pull quotes in large italic with em-dash attribution and generous line spacing. Body text at a refined, readable size with generous leading (1.5× body size).

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Deep charcoal (#2C2C2C) — body text, slightly warmer than pure black
- **Secondary:** Warm ivory (#FAF6EE) — background
- **Accent 1:** Muted sage green (#6B8C6B) — section dividers, drop cap color, subtle accents
- **Accent 2:** Dusty rose (#C4847A) — pull quote rules, special emphasis text
- **Neutral:** Warm mid-gray (#9A9087) — running headers, captions, page numbers

## Layout & Composition

Single wide column of justified body text with generous side margins — the literary magazine single-column essay layout. Pull quotes break out into the wide margin or span across the column. Drop caps anchor the opening of sections. Ornamental dividers (a simple centered asterism *** or three em-dashes) separate sections. Occasional full-bleed literary illustration placeholder in black and white.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Ornamental section dividers (asterism, fleuron, or simple three-dot ornament). Thin rule lines above and below pull quotes. Drop cap first letters sized to 3–4 lines. Optional black-and-white line illustration in editorial style. Running headers with magazine name or essay title at the top margin. Page number at the bottom in small centered type.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Large italic essay title, author name in small caps below, issue date and publication name at bottom margin, no image — purely typographic.
- **Content Slide:** Single-column essay layout with drop cap, body paragraph, pull quote in the right margin, running header at top.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Numbered essay sections presented as titled prose paragraphs, each beginning with a bold section heading in italic small caps.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Statistics woven into body text as prose, with one centered supporting graphic (simple, black-and-white line chart) treated as an editorial figure.
- **Closing Slide:** Final paragraph in larger italic type, ornamental end mark (∎ or fleuron), author attribution, and issue information.

## Tone & Mood

Contemplative, intellectually engaged, and unhurried — the tone of a writer who trusts their audience to stay with them through a developed argument. There is quiet confidence here, not urgency. The audience should feel they are reading something carefully crafted and worth reading slowly.

## Avoid

- Bullet points (use prose paragraphs instead)
- Bold color accents
- Photography or color illustration (black-and-white line illustration only)
- Large display headlines or oversized type
- Digital UI elements of any kind
- Dense data visualization

## Example Prompt

> Create a presentation slide styled as a literary magazine essay page from The New Yorker or London Review of Books. Use a warm ivory background with wide margins, a refined serif font (Caslon or Sabon), justified single-column body text with a large drop cap opening the paragraph, a pull quote in large italic with dusty rose rule lines breaking into the right margin, and a small ornamental section divider. Include a subtle running header in small caps at the top. The feel should be contemplative, intellectually serious, and unhurried — the visual equivalent of long-form literary journalism.
