# Design 01: Bold Editorial Manifesto

**Category:** Editorial & Print-Inspired  
**Style Tags:** editorial, newspaper, manifesto, blueprint, high-contrast, zine  
**Difficulty:** Advanced  
**Best For:** Advanced AI design training decks, product strategy presentations, and agentic workflow documentation. Ideal when the presenter wants to project intellectual authority and design confidence.

---

## Visual Direction

Raw, intelligent, instructional, and slightly rebellious. Feels like it was designed by a strategist, product designer, and newspaper art director working together. High-contrast and intentionally constructed with a tactile quality that feels physical rather than digital. The overall effect should be like a visual blueprint, a tactical field guide, and a bold magazine spread simultaneously.

## Background & Texture

Textured off-white paper background resembling aged paper, scanned newsprint, photocopied pages, faint editorial clippings, and subtle creases. Overlay faint grid lines, blueprint construction marks, crop marks, and alignment guides. The background should look like a working design board or annotated architectural plan.

## Typography

Extremely bold and hierarchical. Oversized condensed black uppercase headlines similar to vintage newspaper mastheads or protest poster typography — headlines dominate and take up significant horizontal space. Secondary serif typeface for explanatory body copy gives an editorial, magazine-like feel. Body text is smaller but legible with strong contrast against the background.

## Color Palette

- **Primary:** Black — dominant typography, shapes, and emphasis
- **Secondary:** Off-white / newsprint beige — background texture
- **Accent 1:** Electric yellow — hand-drawn highlighter marks behind key phrases
- **Accent 2:** Vivid blue — system/process blocks and structural elements
- **Warning/Outcome:** Red — final outcomes, warnings, decisive end states
- **Neutral:** Gray — early-stage or low-confidence steps

## Layout & Composition

Asymmetrical but balanced — some open space alongside oversized type, construction lines, and annotations. Layered composition: paper texture at the bottom, faint grid/newsprint overlays, then large typography, then geometric blocks, then highlights and arrows on top. Strong geometric blocks organize information into rectangular labels, step blocks, and process stages. Leave deliberate breathing room alongside dense information zones.

## Graphic Elements & Decorations

Hand-drawn annotations including arrows, scribbles, circles, X marks, directional lines, and rough underlines communicate motion, critique, progression, or emphasis. Yellow marker highlights behind important phrases should look hand-drawn, slightly uneven, and imperfect. Human figure or object cutouts in black and white with thick yellow outlines create a collage effect. Rectangular cut-out labels pasted onto the slide with flat, high-contrast fills.

## Slide Types & Templates

- **Title Slide:** Large bold headline with one key phrase highlighted in yellow. Human figure or object cutout in black and white with thick yellow outline. Simple black arrows pointing toward the subject or key message. Crop marks in corners.
- **Content Slide:** Massive headline on the left or top, highlighted explanatory paragraph, dark rectangular callout box on the right, arrows connecting concepts to specific audiences or outcomes, minimal blue or red geometric accents.
- **Process/Flow Slide:** Labeled blocks moving upward and to the right, rough yellow zig-zag line suggesting momentum, gray→blue→red color progression representing maturity stages.
- **Data/Chart Slide:** Blueprint-style charts with construction marks and grid paper backgrounds, hand-annotated callouts, rulers and measurement marks framing data.
- **Closing Slide:** Bold manifesto statement, single yellow-highlighted phrase, crop marks framing the entire composition.

## Tone & Mood

Communicates mastery over magic, process over hype, architecture over one-shot prompting, and disciplined iteration. The feel is that of a tactical field guide created by someone who has genuinely earned their insights. Transformative — moving the audience from beginner workflows to production-ready outputs.

## Avoid

- Glossy gradients or soft SaaS visuals
- Rounded cards or pastel status boxes
- Soft drop shadows
- Stock corporate illustrations
- Generic AI futurism or neon cyberpunk aesthetics
- Cluttered dashboards or thin decorative accent borders

## Example Prompt

> Create a presentation slide in a bold editorial manifesto style combining vintage newspaper aesthetics with architectural blueprint construction marks. Use an aged off-white paper texture background with faint grid lines and crop marks, oversized condensed black uppercase headlines, electric yellow hand-drawn highlights behind key phrases, vivid blue geometric process blocks, and red outcome indicators. Add hand-drawn arrows and annotation scribbles throughout. The overall feel should be raw, high-contrast, tactile, and intellectually authoritative — like a training deck designed by a strategist and newspaper art director working together.
